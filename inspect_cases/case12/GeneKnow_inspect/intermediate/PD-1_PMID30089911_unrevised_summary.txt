In metastatic melanoma, PD-1 is expressed on T cells and PD-L1 is expressed on tumor cells and on tumor-derived exosomes. Tumor cell surface PD-L1 and extracellular PD-L1 on released exosomes bind PD-1 on antigen-experienced CD8 T cells and inactivate the CD8 T cells. IFN-γ up-regulates PD-L1 on exosomes and increases exosomal PD-1 binding in a concentration-dependent manner. Exosomal PD-L1 suppresses CD8 T cell function, facilitates tumor growth, and enables tumors to counteract immune pressure at the effector stage. Anti-PD-L1 or PD-1 antibodies block the exosomal PD-L1–PD-1 interaction. In patients, circulating exosomal PD-L1 positively correlates with IFN-γ and changes during anti-PD-1 therapy; early on-treatment increases stratify clinical responders from non-responders and support its use as a predictor for anti-PD-1 therapy.
