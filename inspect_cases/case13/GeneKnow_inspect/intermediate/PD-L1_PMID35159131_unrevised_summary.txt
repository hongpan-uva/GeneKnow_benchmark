In non-small cell lung cancer, PD-L1 in the PD-1/PD-L1 axis serves as a therapeutic target and is targeted alongside CTLA-4 in immune checkpoint therapies. PD-L1 expression in tumor tissue is the preferred biomarker for predicting efficacy of PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors, and high PD-L1 positivity is often accompanied by high T lymphocyte infiltration. A systematic review of 19 studies in advanced NSCLC showed patients with high PD-L1 expression benefited more from single-agent anti-PD-1/PD-L1 drugs than patients with low expression. In NSCLC patients with high tumor mutational burden (≥10 mutations/Mb), CheckMate-227 showed that first-line nivolumab plus ipilimumab significantly prolonged progression-free survival regardless of PD-L1 expression status.
