APOE4 is described as the single greatest genetic risk factor for sporadic Alzheimer’s disease. APOE4 affected major brain cell types in CRISPR/Cas9-edited human iPSC models and in isogenic lines created from APOE3 parental cells. Transcriptional profiling identified hundreds of differentially expressed genes per cell type. Profiling showed synaptic function was most affected in neurons. Profiling showed lipid metabolism was most affected in astrocytes. Profiling showed immune response was most affected in microglia-like cells. APOE4 neurons showed increased synapse number and elevated Aβ42 secretion relative to isogenic APOE3 neurons. APOE4 astrocytes displayed impaired Aβ uptake and cholesterol accumulation. APOE4 microglia-like cells showed altered morphologies that correlated with reduced Aβ phagocytosis. Converting APOE4 to APOE3 in sAD patient-derived and isogenic iPSC-derived brain cell types alleviated most AD-related phenotypes. APOE3 conversion did not reduce Aβ42 secretion from sAD neurons. The reduction in early endosome numbers in APOE3 sAD neurons was not statistically significant despite a trend. It is likely that additional factors in the sAD iPSC-derived cell line confound the AD-related pathologies observed.
