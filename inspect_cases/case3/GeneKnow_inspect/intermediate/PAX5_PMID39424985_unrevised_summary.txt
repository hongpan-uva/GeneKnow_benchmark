In Sirt7−/− pro-B cells transplanted into sublethally irradiated mice, PAX5 deacetylation at K198 was necessary for B cell development. Expression of a deacetylation-mimic Pax5 K198R and, to a lesser extent, Pax5 WT restored repression of a subset of genes upregulated in Sirt7−/− pro-B cells, including Cxcr5 and Tet1. Restoring Pax5 deacetylation rescued splenic B220+ CD19+ GFP+ B cell numbers and restricted lineage plasticity by repressing lineage-inappropriate genes. In Pax5−/− pro-B cells, expression of a constitutively deacetylated Pax5 K198R mutant abrogated T cell potential but failed to restore B cell development. Neither acetylated nor deacetylated Pax5 mimics rescued B cell differentiation, implying both forms are required for lymphopoiesis. The deacetylated mimic overactivated Igh recombination genes and failed to induce normal clonal expansion programs. Binding of Pax5 K198Q and Pax5 K198R to promoters or putative proximal enhancers correlated with induction or silencing of the bound genes. Modulation of acetylation at Pax5 K198 creates a regulatory switch. K198-deacetylated Pax5 potently represses lineage-inappropriate and cytokine signaling genes in pro-B cells. K198-acetylated Pax5 fails to repress lineage-inappropriate genes but efficiently regulates V(D)J and proliferation genes.
