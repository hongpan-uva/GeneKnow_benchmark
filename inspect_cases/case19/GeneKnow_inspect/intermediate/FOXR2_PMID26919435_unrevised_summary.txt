The pediatric CNS molecular group "CNS neuroblastoma with FOXR2 activation" is defined by FOXR2 activation. In CNS NB-FOXR2 tumors FOXR2 is activated and overexpressed relative to other CNS tumors and normal brain. Activation arises from complex genomic rearrangements that produce fusion transcripts retaining the full FOXR2 coding sequence fused to upstream noncoding exons and partners JMJD1C, LOC550643, and JPX. Additional alterations include copy-number changes, deletions between FOXR2 and MAGEH1, an approximately 500 kb upstream deletion potentially fusing FOXR2 to MAGED2, and a mitochondrial insertion in USP51 that creates a novel FOXR2 promoter. These promoter-fusion events suggest FOXR2 activation instigates oncogenic activity. CNS NB-FOXR2 tumors display embryonal architecture and a small-cell phenotype. Tumors frequently show differentiated areas of neuropil, neurocytic cells, or ganglion cells. Individual samples often contain perivascular anuclear zones (vascular pseudorosettes), nuclear palisades, and Homer Wright rosettes. This entity encompasses tumors that would be classified as CNS neuroblastoma or CNS ganglioneuroblastoma in the 2007 WHO classification. CNS NB-FOXR2 tumors nearly uniformly express OLIG2 and the neuronal antigen synaptophysin. One tumor lacked FOXR2 upregulation and instead contained focal MYC amplification.
