KLF4 acts as a tumor suppressor in colorectal cancer and is downregulated in human colorectal cancer tissues compared with normal mucosa. KLF4 inhibited colorectal cancer growth in mice. In colorectal cancer cells KLF4 upregulated p21 and NDRG2 and downregulated cyclinD1, GINS4, and IFITM3 to inhibit cell proliferation. KLF4 deficiency accelerated progression of colitis and colon cancer and was associated with EMT-driven tumor development. MicroRNA-92a and microRNA-10b downregulated KLF4 and promoted colorectal cancer growth, migration, proliferation, and metastasis. In colon cells CDX2 upregulated KLF4 by binding the KLF4 promoter and by reducing histone 3 lysine 4 trimethylation association with the KDM5B demethylase. A clinical trial found that metformin increased KLF4 expression and had a positive effect on colorectal cancer. APTO-253, a targeted inducer of KLF4, halted progression of some advanced tumors in a clinical trial but the program was discontinued after the company stated it inhibited MYC. The passage states that transcription factors are generally considered undruggable and that it is premature to consider KLF4 a general target for cancer therapy.
