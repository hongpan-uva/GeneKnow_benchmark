MyoD1 is an important transcription factor and co-activator of genes responsible for myocyte differentiation. In TAFAZZIN-knockout (TAZ-KO) myoblasts decreased Wnt/β-catenin signaling leads to upregulation of the MyoD1 negative regulator Mohawk (MKX). MKX directly represses MyoD1 transcription. In TAFAZZIN-deficient cells noncanonical Wnt signaling is implicated in dysfunctional MKX–MyoD1 regulation. MyoD1 expression is reported to depend on noncanonical rather than canonical Wnt signaling. Decreased MyoD1 in TAZ-KO myoblasts is associated with reduced myoblast differentiation and myogenesis defects in response to myogenic stimuli. Decreased MyoD1 in TAFAZZIN-deficient myoblasts is proposed to underlie skeletal myopathy in Barth syndrome. Loss of MyoD1 results in muscle regeneration defects in a Duchenne muscular dystrophy mouse model.
