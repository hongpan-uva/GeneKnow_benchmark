CEBPA is described as a master regulator of myeloid differentiation, and its expression peaks in the granulocyte‑monocyte progenitor stage then diminishes during terminal granulocyte differentiation. In APL cell lines NB4 and HT93, CEBPA is required for upregulation of HK3 and KLF5 during ATRA‑induced neutrophil differentiation. CEBPA knockdown in NB4 and HT93 reduced HK3 and KLF5 mRNA after four days of 1 μM ATRA. Ectopic CEBPA expression in HT93 increased HK3 and KLF5 transcription. In K562 lines, activation of full‑length CEBPA‑p42 but not CEBPA‑p30 or CEBPA‑BRM2 induced KLF5. In AML patient cohorts, disrupted CEBPA function correlated with significantly lower HK3 and KLF5 mRNA expression. Wild‑type CEBPA correlated with enhanced HK3 and KLF5 expression, and this correlation held irrespective of single or double CEBPA mutations.
