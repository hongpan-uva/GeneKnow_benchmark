Systematic: In T-cell acute lymphoblastic leukemia (T-ALL), more than 50% of cases are associated with DNA alterations in NOTCH1 or in other genes that lead to enhanced activation of the NOTCH1 pathway. Constitutive expression of the NOTCH1-ΔE variant, which lacks the extracellular domain, leads to overactive NOTCH1 signaling. Overactive NOTCH1 signaling enforces T-cell differentiation and drives development of T-ALL–like disease. In xenotransplant models in sublethally irradiated NSG mice using cord blood cells, only cells expressing wild-type NOTCH1-ΔE generated T-ALL–like leukemias while cells expressing the dimerization-defective NOTCH1-ΔE-R1984A did not. NOTCH1 dimeric signaling was crucial for leukemogenesis of human hematopoietic stem/progenitor cells from cord blood in synthetic models of human T-ALL. A Notch dimerization–dependent gene signature included HES4. HES4 conferred a proliferative advantage in human HSPCs and in patient-derived Notch dimerization–dependent T-ALL xenografts. In human T-ALL cells, HES4 enforced Δ133p53 and blocked proapoptotic p53 targets. HES4 induced BCL2L1 and antiapoptotic BCL‑XL. Cell subsets with Notch dimerization–dependent signatures indirectly correlated with proapoptotic genes and directly associated with markers of poor clinical outcome in primary T-ALL samples. The findings support targeting NOTCH1 dimer signaling or its downstream effectors.
