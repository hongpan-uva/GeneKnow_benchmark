In ACP mice, P3F (Pax3-FOXO1) expression reprogrammed aP2-Cre–labeled cells to acquire muscle stem cell properties capable of muscle regeneration. P3F expression produced tumors in all 24 ACP mice but did not generate fusion-positive rhabdomyosarcoma. Pax3-FOXO1 was present in three ACP tumors and none expressed Myod1 or Myog diagnostic for rhabdomyosarcoma. In most alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma tumors, chromosomal translocations t(2;13) or t(1;13) produce the PAX3-FOXO1 fusion oncoprotein. The fusion joins the PAX3 DNA-binding domain with the transactivation domain of FOXO1. The presence of PAX3-FOXO1 foretells a worse prognosis. PAX3-FOXO1 defines fusion-positive RMS and more accurately categorizes RMS patients versus fusion-negative disease. High-risk fusion-positive RMS can present without an identifiable primary tumor and may show widespread disseminated disease or solely bone marrow involvement.
