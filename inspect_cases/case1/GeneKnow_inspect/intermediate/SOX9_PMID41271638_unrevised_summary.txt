Sox9 is a key transcriptional regulator that specifies astrocytes during development. Sox9 is specifically expressed in naïve astrocytes and is upregulated in reactive astrocytes after brain and spinal cord injury. In naïve animals, Sox9 maintains basal Hk1 levels. Nerve injury triggers Sox9 phosphorylation that enhances nuclear translocation and increases Sox9 transcriptional activity. This modification leads to pathological Hk1 upregulation and promotes aberrant glycolytic flux in astrocytes. Phosphorylated Sox9 in astrocytes induces aberrantly high Hk1 activities. These Hk1 activities affect gene expression via histone lactylation in astrocytes. Phosphorylated Sox9 contributes to the emergence of an Hk1-mediated neuroinflammatory astrocyte cluster and induces an Astro 2 subtype with neurodegenerative and demyelinating nature. These changes promote detrimental astrocyte properties in the dorsal horn and contribute to neuropathic pain development.
