In prostate cancer FOXA1 acts as a pioneer factor that induces open chromatin to enable binding by other transcription factors. FOXA1 directly interacts with the Androgen Receptor and helps shape AR signaling that drives growth and survival of normal prostate and prostate cancer cells. FOXA1 also regulates epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition independently of AR. Mutations in FOXA1 coding sequences and cis-regulatory elements and post-translational modifications such as LSD1-mediated demethylation lead to functional alterations in prostate cancer. FOXA1 mutations occur across stages of prostate cancer and are detectable in cell-free DNA from bodily fluids. Targeted sequencing identified FOXA1 3′ UTR somatic mutations with indels as the dominant alteration. These indels occur in early prostate tumours and are absent from other cancers. Mutation classes show stage specificity, with Forkhead Wing2 coding mutations in localized disease and truncations and structural variants enriched in metastatic disease. Therefore FOXA1 mutation status can inform disease progression and serve as a prostate cancer‑specific biomarker for tracking minimal residual disease and recurrence. Prognostic value and mutant‑targeted inhibitors require further evaluation. FOXA1 is recurrently mutated in its coding sequence in prostate cancer, with reported frequencies up to 9% of primary tumours, 13% of mCRPC, about 25% of NEPC in a 29‑tumour study, and 41% of localized tumours in the Chinese CPGEA cohort, which is higher than Western cohorts.
