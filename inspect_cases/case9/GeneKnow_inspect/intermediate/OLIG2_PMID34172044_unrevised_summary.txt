OLIG2 is a basic-helix-loop-helix transcription factor and a master regulator of oligodendrocyte development in mouse NSCs, OPCs, and immature oligodendrocytes. Knockout of Olig2 in mice led to failure of oligodendroglial lineage generation. Specific deletion of Olig2 in OPCs reduced oligodendrocyte differentiation and caused hypomyelination. Ablation of Olig2 in immature oligodendrocytes induced maturation and promoted myelination and remyelination. Overexpression of Olig2 in neural stem cells promoted generation of OPCs and mature oligodendrocytes. OLIG2 binds protein-coding and lncRNA loci and coincides with H3K27m3/H3K4m3 bivalent marks. Motif analysis indicated cooperation with other transcription factors and OLIG2 can both activate and repress lncRNAs enriched for oligodendrocyte differentiation. OLIG2 binds AU-rich elements in the Olig2 3′ UTR and is involved in post-transcriptional regulation of Olig2. In NSCs OLIG2 binds and represses lnc-OPC expression and this repression likely maintains NSC self-renewal. In OPCs OLIG2 did not bind lnc-OPC's upstream regulatory region and lnc-OPC expression increased. Knocking down lnc-OPC greatly reduced OPC formation from NSCs. Genome-wide OLIG2-bound lncRNAs clustered by expression, and OLIG2 repressed one lncRNA set in NSCs while activating another in OPCs and NFOs. Expression of those groups correlated with oligodendrocyte development and differentiation.
