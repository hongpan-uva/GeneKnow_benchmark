PAX5 mediates B cell lineage commitment and development [1-3]. PAX5 activates B cell-specific gene expression [1,4,5]. Additionally, PAX5 activates B cell-specific signaling proteins, such as CD19 and BLNK, to determine B cell commitment and differentiation [1]. PAX5 activates PAIR elements and induces antisense transcription in the distal V_H cluster, promoting distal V_H-to-D_HJ_H recombination [1]. Pax5 mediates lineage commitment in B cell progenitors [2]. SIRT7 deacetylates Pax5 at K198 and promotes Pax5 stability and transcriptional activity [2]. K198 deacetylation is required for pro‑B commitment because K198 mutants or Sirt7−/− pro‑B cells produced T and NK cells [2]. PAX5 is a B‑cell‑specific activator protein that binds the GINS1 promoter and activates GINS1 transcription [4]. In DLBCL, PAX5 dysregulation increased GINS1 expression, and circ1857 from PAX5 pre‑mRNA stabilized GINS1 mRNA and promoted lymphoma progression [4]. Pax5 is essential for B cell development and in mouse Pax5 expression is regulated by PU.1, IRF4, IRF8, NF-κB and EBF1 [3]. In chicken DT40 cells, Thy28 binds the Pax5 promoter and positively regulates Pax5 [3]. In germinal center B cells, PAX5 promotes AID expression and IgG1 class-switching [5]. PAX5 is SUMO1-conjugated at K257 and is deSUMOylated by SENP1 [5]. K257 SUMOylation lowers PAX5 protein, and blocking K257 SUMOylation or overexpressing PAX5 increases AID and IgG1 class-switching [5].
