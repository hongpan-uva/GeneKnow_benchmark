Article Summary

Systematic elaboration. PAX5 promotes differentiation and survival of mature B cells. In germinal center B cells PAX5 is predominantly SUMO1-conjugated at K257. SENP1 binds PAX5 and mediates deSUMOylation of PAX5 via its catalytic activity at Cys603. SENP1 deficiency increases SUMO1-conjugated PAX5 in induced germinal center B cells. The observation that SENP1 deficiency reduces PAX5 protein in germinal center B cells suggests that deSUMOylation influences PAX5 stability. SUMOylation of PAX5 suppresses AID expression and class-switch recombination. Blocking PAX5 SUMOylation with the K257R mutant increases AID expression in primary B cells and increases class-switching (IgG1+) in transferred B cells. Forced expression of PAX5 or the K257R mutant enhances class-switch recombination in stimulated B cells, and forced expression of PAX5 increases AID expression in SENP1-deficient B cells. Accordingly, SENP1 controls AID expression to regulate class-switch recombination through deSUMOylation of PAX5.

To determine the impact of SUMO1 modification on PAX5, we first analyzed the protein level by immunoblot. Decreased level of PAX5 protein was observed in SENP1-deficient GC B cells ( Fig. 7 A ), whereas the mRNA level of Pax5 was comparable between WT and SENP1-deficient GC B cells ( Fig. 7 B and C ). To further determine the effect of PAX5 SUMOylation on AID expression and CSR in GC B cells, primary B cells were overexpressed with mouse PAX5 (mPAX5) or PAX5 mutant (K257R), which blocks PAX5 SUMOylation. As expected, the frequency of class-switched IgG1 B cells was markedly increased in B cells overexpressing mPAX5 compared to the nontransduced control ( Fig. 7 D ). Intriguingly, B cells overexpressing PAX5 mutant produced even higher class-switched IgG1 ( Fig. 7 D ). In accord with the CSR effect, the expression of AID was increased in B cells overexpressing mPAX5, while overexpression of PAX5 mutant (K257R) in B cells led to a further increase in the AID expression level ( Fig. 7 E ). To further confirm the effect of PAX5 SUMOylation on CSR in GC in vivo, B1-8 hi B cells (CD45.1), which were infected with retrovirus expressing control plasmid, mPAX5, or PAX5 mutant (K257R), were transferred with OT-II CD4+ T cells (CD45.2) to CD45.2 recipient mice, which were subsequently immunized with NP-OVA and LPS. Donor-specific IgG1 + GC B cells were increased in mice that received mPAX5-infected B1-8 hi B cells compared to control mice. However, mice that received PAX5 mutant–infected B1-8 hi B cells generated even more donor-specific IgG1 + GC B cells ( Fig. 7 F ). These results suggest that SUMOylation of PAX5 suppresses the class-switch of GC B cells. Furthermore, we examined whether PAX5 can restore AID expression and CSR in SENP1-deficient B cells. Interestingly, overexpression PAX5 in SENP1 KO B cells restored the amount of IgG1 + B cells, which was comparable to that in WT B cells ( Fig. 7 G ). Consistently, overexpression of PAX5 enhanced AID expression as well in SENP1 KO B cells ( Fig. 7 H ). To further confirm that reduced AID level is responsible for the defect of CSR in SENP1-deficient B cells, both WT and SENP1-deficient B cells were infected with retrovirus expressing AID. Overexpression of AID resulted in an increase of class-switched IgG1+ B cells in both WT and SENP1 KO B cells. Moreover, overexpression of AID successfully rescued the loss of class-switched IgG1+ B cells in the SENP1 KO group ( Fig. 7 I ). These data suggest that the impaired CSR in SENP1 KO B cells is caused by reduced AID expression. Together, these data suggest that SENP1 regulates AID expression to control CSR through deSUMOylation of PAX5.

Regulation of AID expression could directly affect B cell function. In mice deficient of TET enzymes, the Acida super-enhancer was silenced by 5-hydroxymethylcytosine modifications and resulted in defective CSR ( 27 ). PTEN deficiency resulted in hyperphosphorylation of AKT, which is known to inhibit the expression and function of AID and thus cause impaired CSR ( 28 , 29 ). Ascl2, a member of the E protein family, directly regulates AID expression and promotes GC B cell response ( 30 ). Previous studies have shown that PAX5 could bind AID and regulate its expression in B cells ( 31 , 32 ). In particular, PAX5 promotes the differentiation and survival of mature B cells, which is required for humoral immunity ( 33 ). An important question is whether PTMs, especially SUMOylation of PAX5, could regulate its function. Our data revealed a deSUMOylation-dependent regulation of PAX5 in GC B cell response. PAX5 is predominantly conjugated to SUMO1 and regulated by SENP1 at the K257 site. The protein level of PAX5 was diminished in SENP1-deficient GC B cells, suggesting that SENP1-mediated PAX5 deSUMOylation might have an influence on protein stability. Nevertheless, it remains to be studied how the protein level of PAX5 is regulated by SUMOylation. Forced expression of PAX5 or its K257R mutated form enhanced CSR in B cells in response to anti-CD40 and IL-4 stimulation. Recently, PAX5 was reported to regulate AID expression to control CSR in FO B cells by regulating the level of PTEN ( 33 ). However, we did not observe decreased Pten mRNA level in our RNA-seq data. Overexpression of PAX5 could increase the CSR in SENP1-deficient B cells to comparable level in WT B cells. Future studies are needed to understand how AID is regulated by PAX5 in SENP1-deficient GC B. These findings suggest that increase of PAX5 would reverse the impaired humoral immunity caused by SENP1 deficiency.
