GATA1 acts as an erythroid transcription factor [1,2]. GATA1 is an essential regulator of erythropoiesis and terminal erythroid differentiation [2,3]. GATA1 is the master erythroid transcription factor in HSPCs and erythroid differentiation activates ribosomal stress–ZAKα/P38, promoting NLRP1 assembly and inflammasome-mediated GATA1 cleavage [1]. Nlrp1 loss increased erythrocyte counts in zebrafish [1]. Nilotinib inhibition of ZAKα enhanced erythroid differentiation and GATA1 in K562 [1]. GATA1 functions as a transcription factor that modulates autophagy [2]. GATA1's modulation of autophagy regulates erythroid differentiation to generate mature red blood cells [2]. GATA1 is required for erythropoiesis and terminal erythroid differentiation and dose-dependently regulates progenitor survival and cell-cycle via EPO [3]. GATA1 deficiency arrests at the proerythroblast stage and causes apoptosis associated with dyserythropoietic disorders [3]. Tg(gata1:dsRed) zebrafish embryos show dsRed fluorescence in circulating red blood cells [4]. Seventy-five percent of embryos were expected to be fluorescent [4].
