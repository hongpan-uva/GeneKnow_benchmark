PAX5 directs B cell lineage commitment [1-3]. PAX5 functions as a B-cell–specific positive transcription factor that activates B-cell genes [1,4]. PAX5 promotes differentiation of B cells [1,5]. For example, PAX5 activates the B cell–specific signaling proteins CD19 and BLNK to determine B cell commitment and differentiation [1]. PAX5 promotes distal V_H→D_HJ_H recombination by activating chromatin and inducing antisense transcription at PAIR elements [1]. Pax5 directs B cell lineage commitment [2]. SIRT7 deacetylates Pax5 K198 to promote Pax5 stability and transcriptional activity [2]. Dynamic K198 deacetylation is required for B cell development and a deacetylation mimic restored lineage commitment in Pax5−/− and Sirt7−/− pro-B cells [2]. PAX5 functions as a B-cell–specific positive transcription factor that activates GINS1 transcription in B lymphopoiesis [4]. Dysregulated PAX5 was linked to increased GINS1 and DLBCL progression, and circ1857 from PAX5 pre-mRNA stabilizes GINS1 mRNA and promotes lymphoma progression [4]. Pax5 is an essential transcription factor for B cell development [3]. In chicken DT40 cells Thy28 binds the Pax5 promoter and positively regulates Pax5 expression [3]. In Thy28-deficient mice B cell development and Pax5 expression were normal [3]. PAX5 promotes differentiation and survival of mature B cells [5]. In germinal center B cells PAX5 is SUMO1-conjugated at K257 and SENP1 binds and mediates deSUMOylation [5]. SUMOylation of PAX5 suppresses AID expression and class-switch recombination [5].
