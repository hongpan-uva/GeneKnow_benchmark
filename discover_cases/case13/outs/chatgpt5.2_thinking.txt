In NSCLC models, oncogenic EGFR activation (via EGF stimulation, exon 19 deletion, or L858R) was reported to induce tumor-cell PD-L1 (CD274) expression, and in a tumor–PBMC co-culture system PD-L1 upregulation was reported to mediate immune escape via the PD-L1/PD-1 axis, including increased apoptosis of T cells in the co-culture setting. (Chen et al., 2015) In ALK-positive NSCLC, overexpression of the EML4–ALK fusion protein was reported to increase PD-L1 expression, and PD-L1 induction in tumor/immune-cell co-culture was reported to increase T-cell apoptosis; ALK inhibition was reported to reduce PD-L1 and reverse these effects in the experimental systems tested. (Hong et al., 2016) Clinically, first-line pembrolizumab improved progression-free survival and overall survival versus platinum chemotherapy in metastatic NSCLC with PD-L1 tumor proportion score (TPS) ≥50%. (Reck et al., 2016) First-line atezolizumab improved overall survival versus platinum chemotherapy in patients with high PD-L1 expression (IMpower110). (Herbst et al., 2020) In previously treated NSCLC, atezolizumab improved overall survival versus docetaxel in the phase 3 OAK trial. (Rittmeyer et al., 2017) After concurrent chemoradiotherapy for unresectable stage III NSCLC, durvalumab improved progression-free survival versus placebo in PACIFIC, with additional analyses reporting outcomes by tumor PD-L1 expression. (Antonia et al., 2017; Paz-Ares et al., 2020)
