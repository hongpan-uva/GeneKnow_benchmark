PD-L1

PD-L1 serves as the preferred predictive biomarker for immune checkpoint inhibitor efficacy in NSCLC [1-3]. PD-L1 functions as the ligand interacting with PD-1 in the PD-1/PD-L1 immune checkpoint in NSCLC [1,4]. In NSCLC, PD-L1 in the PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint is the preferred biomarker to predict immune checkpoint inhibitor efficacy, and high PD-L1 expression associates with greater benefit from single-agent anti-PD-1/PD-L1 [1]. PD-L1 is expressed in human NSCLC and is a tumor cell-surface predictive biomarker for anti-PD-1/PD-L1 therapy [2]. In EGFR-mutant NSCLC, EGFR signaling induces PD-L1 [2]. YAP regulates PD-L1 transcription and upregulates PD-L1 in gefitinib-resistant cells [2]. In NSCLC tumor cells, PD-L1 activates Jak2 and Stat3 phosphorylation, leading to increased IL-6 and other cytokine expression [5]. In patients with NSCLC, PD-L1 status modulated IL-6/Jak/Stat3 pathway enrichment and high IL-6 associated with poor ICI outcomes in PD‑L1‑high tumors [5]. In oncogene‑addicted non‑small cell lung cancer, PD‑L1 expression impacted clinical outcomes from immune checkpoint blockade [3]. BRAF‑mutant tumors exhibited high PD‑L1 and high tumor mutational burden [3]. High tumor mutational burden may increase ICB sensitivity and was associated with superior benefit [3]. In NSCLC, PD‑L1 interacts with PD‑1 [4]. HRS mediates sorting of PD‑L1 into exosomes, which restricts CD8+ T cell anti‑tumor functions [4]. LAMTOR1 suppresses HRS‑mediated secretion of exosomal PD‑L1 in NSCLC, and this suppression improves CD8+ T cell function [4].
