MYOD1

MyoD1 functions as a transcription factor that drives muscle cell differentiation [1-3]. MyoD1 is a transcription factor that drives myocyte differentiation [1]. In TAFAZZIN‑knockout myoblasts, Mohawk (MKX) is upregulated and inhibits MyoD1 transcription via disrupted noncanonical Wnt signaling [1]. As a result, TAZ‑KO myoblast differentiation is reduced [1]. BC3H‑1 cells lacking endogenous MyoD1 were transfected with MyoD1 [2]. The introduced MyoD1 localized to the nucleus [2]. BD lines formed long multinucleated myotubes instead of parental mononucleated MHC‑expressing cells, yet both expressed the same sarcomeric MHC isoform pattern [2]. 1,25‑Dihydroxyvitamin D induced VDR‑mediated transcription that upregulated MYOD1 expression in C2C12 myoblasts [4]. In embryonic myogenesis, MYOD1 is expressed when PAX3+ precursors commit into myoblasts and activates muscle differentiation by binding E‑box motifs in muscle gene promoters [3]. MYOD1 activation precedes differentiation into MYOG‑expressing myocytes that fuse into myotubes and mature into myofibers [3]. Researchers designed qPCR primers including MYOD1 for the Atlantic mackerel genome and used the primers to characterize genotypes of the Mack skeletal muscle cell line [5].
