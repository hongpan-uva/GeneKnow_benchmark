MYOD1 is a master myogenic regulatory factor that directly controls muscle cell lineage specification and differentiation through sequence-specific transcriptional activation of muscle genes. Ectopic expression of MYOD1 converts non-muscle cells such as fibroblasts into myoblast-like cells, demonstrating its sufficiency to initiate the skeletal muscle program (Davis et al., 1987). MYOD1 binds E-box motifs (CANNTG) in regulatory regions of muscle-specific genes and activates transcription of targets including muscle structural proteins and other myogenic regulators (Weintraub et al., 1991). Genome-wide studies show that MYOD1 occupies enhancers and promoters of muscle genes and recruits chromatin-remodeling complexes, facilitating a permissive chromatin state during myogenic differentiation (Cao et al., 2010). MYOD1 also cooperates with other transcription factors such as MEF2 to synergistically activate muscle gene expression programs required for terminal differentiation (Molkentin et al., 1995). In vivo, loss-of-function studies indicate that MYOD1 contributes to proper skeletal muscle development, although partial redundancy with MYF5 allows myogenesis to proceed with altered kinetics in knockout models (Rudnicki et al., 1993). Additionally, MYOD1 regulates cell cycle withdrawal during differentiation by inducing expression of cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitors, linking lineage specification to cell cycle control (Halevy et al., 1995). Together, these findings establish MYOD1 as a central transcriptional regulator that initiates, coordinates, and stabilizes the muscle cell differentiation program through direct gene activation and chromatin modulation.
