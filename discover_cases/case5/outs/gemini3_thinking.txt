MYOD1 is a master transcription factor that serves as a primary determinant of skeletal muscle lineage specification and differentiation (Hernández-Hernández et al., 2017). As a basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) protein, it heterodimerizes with E-proteins to recognize and bind E-box motifs ($CANNTG$) within the regulatory regions of muscle-specific genes (Battistelli et al., 2022). This binding capacity allows MYOD1 to reprogram diverse non-muscle somatic cells, such as fibroblasts, directly into the myogenic lineage (Battistelli et al., 2022). Beyond early specification, MYOD1 facilitates the assembly of active muscle enhancers by recruiting chromatin-remodeling enzymes and histone-modifying proteins, establishing the epigenetic landscape necessary for myogenesis (Blum & Dynlacht, 2013). It acts hierarchically with other myogenic regulatory factors, such as myogenin, which is required for the subsequent assembly of the transcriptional machinery on differentiation-specific genes (Adhikari et al., 2021). Additionally, MYOD1 functions as a clock amplifier in skeletal muscle, synergistically interacting with core circadian factors like BMAL1 and CLOCK to regulate the amplitude of clock-controlled genes (Hodge et al., 2019). Recent research further demonstrates that MYOD1 orchestrates systemic energy homeostasis by mediating muscle-fat crosstalk through the secretion of the lipokine DLPC (Hu et al., 2023).
