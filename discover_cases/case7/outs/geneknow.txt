Nkx2-1 acts as a regulator that promotes and maintains the AT2 cell program and identity [1-5]. Nkx2-1 regulates respiratory epithelial fate decisions in the alveolar epithelium within a network of transcriptional regulators [4,5]. For example, Nkx2-1 is implicated as a central regulator of Wnt-responsive AT2 (AEP) cells [1]. Nkx2-1 ablation in AEPs in vitro caused irreversible acquisition of a stressed transitional state and uncontrolled proliferation [1]. Nkx2-1 ablation in AEPs in vivo disrupted alveolar structure [1]. CEBPA recruits NKX2-1 to co-bound sites in AT2 cells, promoting and maintaining the AT2 program [2]. In Cebpa mutant mature AT2 cells, NKX2-1 binding at AT2 peaks decreases [2]. Without CEBPA, neonatal but not mature AT2 cells reactivate the SOX9 program [2]. NKX2-1 in perinatal lungs acts in AT2 cells to control surfactant lipid and protein gene expression [4]. PRDM3 and PRDM16 control chromatin accessibility at NKX2-1 targets [4]. Joint loss of PRDM3 and PRDM16 causes AT2 loss, partial AT1 fate, and perinatal death [4]. NKX2-1 regulates respiratory epithelial fate decisions in the alveolar epithelium, functioning within a network alongside YAP, TEAD, KLF5, and NFIB [5]. Deletion of NKX2-1 in AT2 cells leads to loss of respiratory epithelial identity resembling gastrointestinal cells [5]. In Lin POS organoids induced to differentiate into alveolar type II (AT2) cells, NKX2-1 was upregulated [3].
