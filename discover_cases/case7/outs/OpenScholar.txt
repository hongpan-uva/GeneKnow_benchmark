## NKX2-1 — alveolar type II (AT2) cells

**Query:** What functional relationships between NKX2-1 (TTF-1) and alveolar type II (AT2) cells have been directly reported in peer-reviewed research articles?

NKX2-1 is involved in the regulation of genes involved in breathing, innate defense, and tumorigenesis [2]. Specifically, NKX2-1 binds to genes involved in these processes, including SFTPB, LAMP3, SFTPA1, SFTPA2, MYBPH, and LMO3 [2]. Moreover, NKX2-1 has been shown to regulate the expression of genes necessary for neoplastic transformation in Kras-mutant alveolar type II cells [7]. Additionally, LKB1, a tumor suppressor gene, has been found to regulate lineage-specific transcription factors, including NKX2-1, to constrain lung tumor development through enforced differentiation [3]. Furthermore, the epigenetic maintenance of NKX2-1 is crucial for the control of facultative progenitor activity in AEPs [0].
