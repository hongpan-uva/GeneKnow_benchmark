NKX2-1 (also known as TTF-1 or thyroid transcription factor-1) is a homeodomain transcription factor essential for lung development and the maintenance of alveolar type II (AT2) cell identity and function. NKX2-1 directly regulates the expression of surfactant protein genes, including *SFTPA*, *SFTPB*, *SFTPC*, and *SFTPD*, which are hallmark products of mature AT2 cells (Boggaram, 2009). During lung morphogenesis, NKX2-1 is required for the specification of the distal lung epithelial progenitors that give rise to AT2 cells, and its conditional deletion in the murine lung epithelium leads to a loss of AT2 cell identity with cells adopting a more gastrointestinal-like fate (Snyder et al., 2013). NKX2-1 cooperates with other transcription factors, notably FOXA1/FOXA2, to maintain AT2-specific gene expression programs, and its chromatin-binding landscape in AT2 cells differs from that in airway cells, reflecting cell-type-specific regulatory functions (Little et al., 2021). In the context of lung adenocarcinoma, loss of NKX2-1 in AT2-derived tumor cells promotes de-differentiation and lineage switching, underscoring its role as a lineage-survival factor for the AT2 state (Snyder et al., 2013). NKX2-1 expression levels also modulate AT2 cell self-renewal and differentiation into alveolar type I cells during injury repair (Little et al., 2021).
