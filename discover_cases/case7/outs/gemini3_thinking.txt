NKX2-1 (also known as Thyroid Transcription Factor-1) is the master transcriptional regulator essential for the specification, functional maintenance, and identity of Alveolar Type 2 (AT2) cells. During development, NKX2-1 is required for the initial formation of the lung bud and subsequent alveolar differentiation; its absence results in a complete failure of lung morphogenesis and the loss of distal epithelial cell types (Minoo et al., 1999). In mature AT2 cells, NKX2-1 directly activates the transcription of genes critical for surfactant homeostasis, including **Surfactant Proteins A, B, and C** (*Sftpa*, *Sftpb*, *Sftpc*), and the lipid transporter **ABCA3** (Bohinski et al., 1994; Besnard et al., 2007). Beyond functional regulation, NKX2-1 is mandatory for maintaining AT2 lineage fidelity; its conditional deletion in adult cells results in the loss of pulmonary markers and a rapid transdifferentiation into a gastric-like mucosal phenotype (Little et al., 2013). Furthermore, NKX2-1 is necessary for the regenerative capacity of the alveolar epithelium, as it governs the ability of AT2 cells to proliferate and transition into Alveolar Type 1 (AT1) cells following acute lung injury (Little et al., 2021). Essentially, NKX2-1 acts as the genetic anchor that prevents the AT2 cell from drifting into non-pulmonary identities.
