In the developing mouse lung, **Nkx2-1 protein is present in distal epithelial cells and is restricted to bronchiolar and alveolar type II (AT2) cells by late gestation (E19.5)** (Tagne et al., 2012). **In vivo ChIP-based profiling of embryonic lung identified Nkx2-1 occupancy at regulatory regions of surfactant genes (including *Sftpa* and *Sftpb*)**, consistent with a direct transcriptional relationship between Nkx2-1 and AT2-associated surfactant programs (Tagne et al., 2012). Mechanistically, **thyroid transcription factor-1 (TTF-1; encoded by *NKX2-1*) directly targets the surfactant protein B promoter in reporter and DNA-binding assays**, supporting direct transcriptional activation of an AT2 effector gene (*SFTPB/SP-B*) by NKX2-1 (Bohinski & Di Lauro, 1994). **TTF-1 is also sufficient to mediate transcriptional activation of the lung-specific *SFTPC/SP-C* promoter in transfection assays**, linking NKX2-1 activity to another canonical AT2 marker gene (Kelly et al., 1996). In adult alveolar epithelium, **both AT1 and AT2 cells express NKX2-1, and conditional loss-of-function experiments show NKX2-1 is required for alveolar cell fate regulation with accompanying changes in chromatin accessibility** (Little et al., 2021); additionally, **alveolar epithelial progenitors require Nkx2-1 to maintain a progenitor-specific epigenomic state during homeostasis and regeneration** (Toth et al., 2023).
