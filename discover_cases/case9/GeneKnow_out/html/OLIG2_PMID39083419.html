Article Summary

In Opalin conditional knockout (cKO) mice, Olig2+ cell numbers in the corpus callosum were decreased compared with control mice. Among Olig2+ cells in the corpus callosum of Opalin cKO mice, the percentage of CC1+ mature oligodendrocytes was reduced. In Opalin icKO mice, the ratio of CC1+/Olig2+ cells to Olig2+ cells in the corpus callosum was significantly decreased.

To verify whether OL differentiation was affected by OPALIN deletion, we conducted BrdU birth-dating experiments. BrdU was injected intraperitoneally into mice at P7 once a day for three consecutive days, and CC1/BrdU costaining was subsequently performed on brain sections prepared from the mice at P14 ( Fig. 4 F ). We found that the ratio of BrdU+/CC1+ cells to CC1+ cells was significantly decreased in the Opalin cKO mice ( Fig. 4 G and H ), indicating that OL differentiation was impaired. We also conducted BrdU pulse-labeling experiments to investigate whether OPALIN deletion affected OPC proliferation. BrdU was injected into the mice at P7, and brain tissues were collected 1 h later. The ratio of BrdU+/PDGFRα+ cells to PDGFRα+ cells was not altered in the Opalin cKO mice ( Fig. 3 K ). The ratio of Ki67+/Olig2+ cells to Olig2+ cells did not change in the Opalin cKO mice ( Fig. 4 I and J ). Overall, these results indicate that OPALIN is critical for OL differentiation but not OPC proliferation.
