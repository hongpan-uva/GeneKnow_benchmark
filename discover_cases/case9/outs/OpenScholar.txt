## OLIG2 — oligodendrocytes, oligodendroglia, or oligodendrocyte precursor cells (OPCs)

**Query:** What functional relationships between OLIG2 and oligodendrocytes, oligodendroglia, or oligodendrocyte precursor cells (OPCs) have been directly reported in peer-reviewed research articles?

OLIG2 plays a crucial role in oligodendrocyte development and function, as it is expressed throughout the oligodendrocyte lineage and is necessary for oligodendrocyte specification and differentiation [0]. OLIG2 promotes the differentiation of oligodendrocyte progenitor cells (OPCs) into oligodendrocytes [0]. OLIG2 regulates the expression of myelin-associated genes in oligodendrocytes [2] and can recruit the tri-methyltransferase SETDB1 to the Sox11 gene, leading to its repression during the differentiation of OPCs into immature oligodendrocytes [2]. OLIG2 has opposing functions on different stages of oligodendrocyte development. It is required for the specification and differentiation of oligodendrocytes, but its deletion in immature oligodendrocytes can enhance their maturation and myelination [5]. However, this effect is cell-type specific, as OLIG2 is not required for the maturation of mature oligodendrocytes [5]. OLIG2 regulates the expression of long non-coding RNAs (lncRNAs) in oligodendrocytes [3]. These lncRNAs play a role in oligodendrocyte development and differentiation. OLIG2 can also regulate its own expression through its 3' untranslated region (UTR) [3]. In addition, OLIG2 can regulate the survival of oligodendrocyte progenitor cells (OPCs) through the DDX20-p53 axis [4]. DDX20 is a protein that is indispensable for the survival of NPCs and OPCs, and OLIG2 contributes to NPC proliferation through DDX20 protein stabilization [4]. Overall, OLIG2 plays a critical role in the development, differentiation, and function of oligodendrocytes, and its dysregulation can lead to myelination defects and demyelinating diseases [5]. OLIG2 is also involved in the regulation of myelin gene expression during myelination [2]. Furthermore, it can recruit the tri-methyltransferase SETDB1 to the Sox11 gene, leading to its repression during the differentiation of OPCs into immature oligodendrocytes [2]. The development of OLIG2-OPCs has been explored for the treatment of ischemic stroke [6]. A stable, chemically defined protocol has been developed to generate OPCs/OLs with partial inhibition of Shh activity by GANT61 from hiPSCs and sequentially induced the expression of the single TF Olig2 [6]. Olig2-OPC transplantation may be an ideal alternative approach for ischemic stroke rehabilitation therapy [6]. In summary, OLIG2 plays a crucial role in oligodendrocyte development, differentiation, and function, and its regulation of myelin gene expression is essential for proper myelination.
