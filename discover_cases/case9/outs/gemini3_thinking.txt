OLIG2 is a basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) transcription factor that serves as a critical determinant of oligodendrocyte lineage specification and maturation. It is essential for the initial formation of oligodendrocyte precursor cells (OPCs); genetic ablation of **Olig2** in mice leads to a complete failure of oligodendrocyte development in both the spinal cord and forebrain (Lu et al., 2002). Within the ventral spinal cord, OLIG2 functions within the pMN domain to establish the oligodendrocyte lineage while simultaneously repressing alternative astrocytic and neuronal fates (Mizuguchi et al., 2001). Beyond specification, OLIG2 continues to regulate the progression of OPCs into mature, myelinating oligodendrocytes by directly promoting the expression of key transcription factors such as **SOX10** (Kuspert et al., 2007). Furthermore, OLIG2 interacts with **NKX2.2** to coordinate the temporal transition from progenitor proliferation to terminal differentiation (Zhou et al., 2001). In the adult central nervous system, OLIG2 remains necessary for the maintenance of the oligodendrocyte identity and the repair of myelin following demyelinating injury (Mei et al., 2013). Essentially, without OLIG2, the central nervous system would lack the "insulation" required for efficient neural signaling.
