ESR1

ESR1 mediates estrogen-independent ER transcriptional activity [1,2]. ESR1 mediates endocrine therapy resistance [2,3]. Specifically, ESR1 in-frame fusions in ER+ breast cancer exclude the ligand-binding domain and drive estrogen-independent ER transcription [1]. ESR1 Y537S mutations drive estrogen-independent metabolic rewiring with elevated TCA activity [1]. PROTAC ARV-471 degrades Y537S/D538G mutants and inhibits tumor growth [1]. In long-term estrogen-deprived ER-positive breast cancer models, ESR1 acquired Y537C or Y537S mutations [2]. The mutations had enriched chromatin binding and enhanced estrogen-independent transcriptional activity [2]. The mutations arise during estrogen withdrawal and mediate the most common mechanism of endocrine therapy resistance [2]. Baseline ESR1 Y537S predicted poor outcomes and Y537C predicted good outcomes in fulvestrant-treated breast cancer [4]. ESR1 F404 contributed to fulvestrant binding via pi-stacking [4]. F404 mutations in cis with activating ESR1 mutations disrupted the pi-stacking bond and conferred fulvestrant resistance [4]. In ER+ breast cancer, activating ESR1 mutations arise in metastatic tumours after aromatase inhibitor exposure [3]. The mutations persist and drive acquired resistance to endocrine therapy [3]. The mutations specify tumours retaining ER dependence [3]. Elacestrant improved PFS in ESR1-mutant advanced disease [3]. In HR-positive HER2-negative metastatic breast cancer, patients whose tumors harbor ESR1 mutations are being treated with novel selective estrogen receptor degraders (SERDs) as the evolving standard of care [5].
