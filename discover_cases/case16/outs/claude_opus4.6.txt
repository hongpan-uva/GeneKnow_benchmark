ESR1 (Estrogen Receptor Alpha) encodes the estrogen receptor α (ERα), a ligand-activated transcription factor that mediates estrogen signaling and plays a central role in the development and progression of breast cancer. Approximately 70–80% of breast cancers express ERα, and its expression status is a primary determinant of endocrine therapy eligibility, including treatment with tamoxifen and aromatase inhibitors (Hayashi & Kimura, 2015). Activating mutations in ESR1, particularly Y537S and D538G in the ligand-binding domain, have been identified in metastatic, endocrine-resistant breast cancers and confer constitutive, ligand-independent receptor activity (Jeselsohn et al., 2015). ESR1 gene amplification has also been reported in a subset of breast tumors and is associated with increased ERα protein expression and endocrine therapy sensitivity, though its frequency and clinical significance remain debated (Holst et al., 2007). ESR1 promoter methylation has been linked to loss of ERα expression in ER-negative breast cancers, suggesting epigenetic silencing as one mechanism underlying receptor-negative status (Lapidus et al., 1996). Detection of ESR1 mutations in circulating tumor DNA has emerged as a clinically relevant biomarker for monitoring resistance to aromatase inhibitors in advanced breast cancer (Schiavon et al., 2015).
