ESR1 encodes estrogen receptor-α (ERα); ER cDNA cloned from the ER-positive breast cancer cell line MCF-7 produced an estradiol-binding receptor when expressed in heterologous cells, establishing ESR1/ERα as the molecular mediator of estrogen binding in this model (Greene et al., 1986). In ERα-positive MCF-7 breast cancer cells, ERα signaling induced by 17β-estradiol drives coordinated downstream expression changes consistent with ERα-dependent proliferative signaling programs (Drabovich et al., 2016). Mechanistically in MCF-7 cells, ERα was reported to mediate proliferation through a p21/PCNA/E2F1-dependent pathway, and ERα knockdown altered proliferation markers and cell-cycle regulators in that experimental context (Liao et al., 2014). In metastatic, hormone-resistant ER-positive breast cancer, recurrent ESR1 ligand-binding domain (LBD) mutations were identified in patient tumors, and functional assays in the same study showed these mutations can confer ligand-independent ER activity and reduced sensitivity to estrogen deprivation (Toy et al., 2013). Independently, activating ESR1 LBD mutations were detected in hormone-resistant metastatic breast cancers from treated patients, supporting ESR1 mutation as a tumor-associated alteration in this setting (Robinson et al., 2013). The specific ESR1 D538G mutation was experimentally shown to display constitutive transcriptional activity and, when overexpressed in breast cancer cells, to enhance proliferation and confer resistance to tamoxifen (Merenbakh-Lamin et al., 2013). ESR1 gene amplification has also been examined in primary breast cancer cohorts and was reported to associate with patient outcome in that study (Markiewicz et al., 2013).
