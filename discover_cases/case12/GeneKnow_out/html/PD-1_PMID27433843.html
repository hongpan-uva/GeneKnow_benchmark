Article Summary

In patients with melanoma, anti–programmed death 1 (PD-1) therapy produces objective responses, and approximately 75% of these responses are durable and last for years. Defects in pathways involved in interferon-receptor signaling and in antigen presentation were associated with acquired resistance to PD-1 blockade immunotherapy in patients with melanoma. Approximately 25% of patients with melanoma who had an objective response to PD-1 blockade experienced disease progression at a median follow-up of 21 months. Delayed relapses have been noted long after initial objective tumor regression despite continuous therapy.
