Programmed cell death protein 1 (PD-1) is an inhibitory receptor targeted therapeutically in advanced melanoma with monoclonal antibodies that block PD-1 signaling. In a first-in-human study that included patients with metastatic melanoma, treatment with the anti-PD-1 antibody nivolumab produced objective tumor responses in a subset of patients, with some responses described as durable. (Topalian et at., 2012) In the randomized phase 3 KEYNOTE-006 trial in advanced melanoma, pembrolizumab (anti-PD-1) prolonged progression-free survival and overall survival compared with ipilimumab. (Robert et al., 2015) In the phase 3 trial comparing nivolumab (anti-PD-1), nivolumab plus ipilimumab, and ipilimumab in untreated advanced melanoma, nivolumab alone or combined with ipilimumab significantly improved progression-free survival versus ipilimumab. (Larkin et al., 2015) In paired tumor biopsies from patients with metastatic melanoma treated with pembrolizumab, responders showed proliferation of intratumoral CD8+ T cells during treatment that correlated with radiographic tumor size reduction, and pretreatment biopsies from responders had higher densities of CD8+, PD-1+, and PD-L1+ cells at the invasive margin and within tumors. (Tumeh et al., 2014)
