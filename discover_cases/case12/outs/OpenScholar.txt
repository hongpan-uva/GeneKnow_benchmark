## PD-1 — melanoma

**Query:** What functional relationships between PD-1 (PDCD1) and melanoma have been directly reported in peer-reviewed research articles?

PD-1 (PDCD1) has been shown to play a crucial role in the regulation of the immune response in melanoma [2]. Studies have reported various functional relationships between PD-1 and melanoma, including its expression on T cells, tumor cells, and tumor-associated macrophages, as well as its role in tumor growth, immune evasion, and response to immunotherapy. **Association between PD-1 expression and melanoma prognosis** High expression of PDCD1 in melanoma is associated with better overall survival (OS) and disease-specific survival (DSS) in certain cancer types, such as skin cutaneous melanoma and uterine corpus endometrial carcinoma [2]. However, high expression of PDCD1 in uveal melanoma and brain lower-grade glioma is associated with poorer OS and DSS [2]. **Tumor cell-intrinsic PD-1 expression** PD-1 expression on T cells has been shown to be negatively regulated by the PD-1/PD-L1/PD-L2 axis, which is a common feature of canine melanoma [3]. High expression of PD-1 and PD-L1 correlates with increased numbers of CD3+ cells [3]. Additionally, the high level of IBA1+ cells in melanomas with low PD-1 expression and low CD3+ cells levels suggest that the expression of checkpoint molecules is modulated by interactions between T cells and cancer cells rather than histiocytes [3]. **Germline variation and PD-1 expression** Germline variation in the PDCD1 gene has been associated with poorer OS in patients with metastatic melanoma treated with anti-PD-1 monotherapy [5]. The PDCD1 804C > T (rs2227981) SNP is associated with decreased gene expression and a reduced fraction of PD-1+CD4+ T cells [5]. This suggests that germline genetics may significantly impact immune responses after ICIs. **PD-1 blockade and adaptive immune resistance** PD-1 blockade has been shown to induce responses by inhibiting adaptive immune resistance [7]. Pre-existing CD8+ T cells that are negatively regulated by the PD-1/PD-L1 immune inhibitory axis may predict response to therapy [7]. A predictive model based on CD8 expression at the invasive margin and validated in an independent cohort of patients [7]. **Type I interferon signaling and PD-1 expression** Type I interferon signaling has been shown to induce PD-1 in melanoma cells via the IFNAR pathway, leading to reversal of ICB efficacy [8]. IFNAR1 or JAK/STAT inhibition suppresses melanoma-PD-1 expression and disrupts ICB efficacy in preclinical models [8]. **MyD88/IL-1R axis and PD-1 expression** The MyD88/IL-1R axis regulates programmed cell death (PD)-1 expression on tumor-associated macrophages (TAMs) by promoting recruitment of NF-κBp65 to the Pdcd1 promoter [9]. A combinatorial immunotherapy approach combining the MyD88 inhibitor with anti–PD-1 blockade elicited strong antitumor effects [9].
