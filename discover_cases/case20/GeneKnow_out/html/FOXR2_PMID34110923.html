Article Summary

FOXR2 is essential in neuroblastoma. It defines an activated subset and is an independent prognostic factor associated with unfavorable clinical outcome. In neuroblastoma, FOXR2 stabilizes MYCN protein, indicating an alternative mechanism to MYCN amplification. MYCN stabilization by FOXR2 may explain the poor clinical outcome and provides crucial knowledge for targeted treatment of FOXR2‑activated neuroblastoma. The mechanism of FOXR2 activation in peripheral neuroblastoma remains elusive because gene fusions driving FOXR2 expression were rarely detected, in contrast to CNS neuroblastoma. Poor survival of the FOXR2 group was observed in the NB97 and NB2004 RNA‑seq studies, and similar responses to different induction or consolidation treatment arms make protocol differences an unlikely explanation. Nearly identical 5‑ and 10‑year event‑free survival and progression‑free survival for FOXR2‑expressing neuroblastoma indicate that primarily early events occur in this group.
