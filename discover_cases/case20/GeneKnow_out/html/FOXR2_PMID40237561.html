Article Summary

The passages state that FOXR2 activation is regarded as pathognomonic for CNS neuroblastoma (NB). FOXR2 overexpression was observed among embryonal tumors and included 11 CNS NB-FOXR2 cases, six pineoblastoma cases (five PB-FOXR2 and one pineal anlage tumor), and one medulloblastoma. Except for a single SHH-activated, TP53-mutant medulloblastoma, the embryonal tumors with FOXR2 overexpression, including the CNS NB-FOXR2 cases, did not harbor other pathogenic or likely pathogenic genetic alterations. All CNS NB-FOXR2 were cerebral cortical tumors. Other CNS tumors with FOXR2 overexpression show regional predilections depending on the specific tumor type. The results support that CNS tumors with FOXR2 overexpression exhibit diverse histopathologic, molecular, and clinical features.
