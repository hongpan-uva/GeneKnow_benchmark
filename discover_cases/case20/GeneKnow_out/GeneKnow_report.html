PAX3-FOXO1

Core roles of PAX3-FOXO1 include acting as an oncogenic fusion transcription factor [1-3]. Core roles also include defining or characterizing fusion-positive alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma [2-5]. In ACP mice PAX3-FOXO1 reprogrammed aP2-Cre labeled cells into muscle stem-like cells and drove diverse tumors lacking fusion-positive rhabdomyosarcoma [4]. Translocations t(2;13) or t(1;13) generate PAX3-FOXO1, which foretells poor prognosis [4]. A t(2;13) translocation produces PAX3-FOXO1 in alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma [5]. PAX3-FOXO1 cooperates with additional molecular changes to promote oncogenic transformation and tumorigenesis [5]. PAX3-FOXO1 expression is restricted to RMS tumor cells and provides a specific therapeutic target [5]. PAX3-FOXO1 fuses the DNA-binding domain of PAX3 with the transactivation domain of FOXO1 in a subset of rhabdomyosarcoma tumors [1]. PAX3-FOXO1 expression characterizes higher-risk, more frequently metastatic tumors [1]. PAX3-FOXO1 expression correlates with worse outcomes for localized disease [1]. A t(2;13) translocation in alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma produces an in-frame PAX3-FOXO1 fusion [2]. PAX3-FOXO1 contains PAX3 DNA-binding domains fused to the FOXO1 transactivation domain and thereby alters transcription [2]. FP-RMS cells are addicted to PAX3-FOXO1 [2]. PAX3-FOXO1 highly binds a super-enhancer within the ALK locus [3]. PAX3-FOXO1 expression is crucial for survival and proliferation of fusion-positive RMS [3].
