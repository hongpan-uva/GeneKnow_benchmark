## PAX3-FOXO1 — rhabdomyosarcoma

**Query:** What functional relationships between PAX3-FOXO1 (also known as PAX3-FKHR) and rhabdomyosarcoma have been directly reported in peer-reviewed research articles?

PAX3-FOXO1 (PAX3-FKHR) is the fusion protein produced by the genomic translocation that characterizes the alveolar subtype of rhabdomyosarcoma, a pediatric sarcoma with a myogenic phenotype [1]. Research has shown that PAX3-FOXO1 is an aberrant but functional transcription factor that retains PAX3-DNA-binding activity and functionally overlaps PAX3 function, while also disturbing it, particularly its role in myogenic differentiation [1]. PAX3-FOXO1 has been shown to interfere with normal FOXO function, affecting FOXO-family member trans-activation capability and the FOXO-dependent TGF-β response [1]. This suggests that PAX3-FOXO1 may contribute to tumor formation by inhibiting the tumor suppressor activities characteristic of both FOXO family members and TGF-β pathways. Furthermore, PAX3-FOXO1 has been found to reprogram mouse and human endothelial progenitors to form functional myogenic stem cells capable of regenerating injured muscle fibers [0]. Additionally, PAX3-FOXO1 regulates B7-H3 expression and is associated with multiple pathways related to an aggressive phenotype in ARMS, such as cell migration and myogenic differentiation block [2]. PAX3-FOXO1 uses its activation domain to recruit CBP/p300 and shape RNA Pol2 cluster distribution, which is essential for its oncogenic transcriptional activity [3]. Mutants of the cysteine in the activation domain of P3F reduce aRMS cell proliferation and induce cellular differentiation. Moreover, PAX3-FOXO1 directly binds to FOXF1 enhancers and induces FOXF1 gene expression, which is required for FP-RMS tumorigenesis [5]. FOXF1 cooperates with PAX3-FOXO1 and E-box transcription factors MYOD1 and MYOG to regulate FP-RMS-specific gene expression. Cyclin-dependent kinase 4 (Cdk4) phosphorylates and positively regulates PAX3-FOXO1, suggesting that inhibition of Cdk4 activity may be a promising avenue for developing therapy for ARMS [6]. Inhibition of Cdk4 has been shown to inhibit the transcriptional activity of PAX3-FOXO1 in ARMS cell line Rh30 [6]. Additionally, CRISPR-Cas9 can be used to model the effects of cancer fusion genes in mice, including the PAX3-Foxo1 chromosomal translocation in alveolar rhabdomyosarcoma [7]. A specific example of the functional relationship between PAX3-FOXO1 and rhabdomyosarcoma is the study on P3F expression in aP2-Cre expressing cells, which reprograms endothelial progenitors to functional myogenic stem cells capable of regenerating injured muscle fibers [0]. Another example is the study on PAX3-FOXO1 knockdown, which downregulates B7-H3 expression and induces myogenic differentiation in ARMS cells [2]. Furthermore, the study on PAX3-FOXO1 and FOXF1 shows that FOXF1 cooperates with PAX3-FOXO1 to regulate FP-RMS-specific gene expression and is required for FP-RMS tumorigenesis [5]. In conclusion, PAX3-FOXO1 plays a critical role in the development and progression of rhabdomyosarcoma, and its functional relationships with other proteins and pathways have been extensively studied in peer-reviewed research articles [2]. Understanding these relationships is crucial for developing targeted therapies for this aggressive form of cancer [4].
