RUNX2 acts as a master regulator of osteoblast differentiation [1-3]. RUNX2 directs multipotent mesenchymal cells to osteoblast-lineage cells [2,3]. RUNX2 in osteoblasts contains an IDR that phase-separates into condensates and mediates long-range regulation between the rs4683184 enhancer and the XCR1 promoter [4]. RUNX2 preferentially binds the rs4683184-A allele and this binding increases enhancer activity and XCR1 mRNA and protein [4]. RUNX2 modulates transcriptional activation and multiple signaling pathways [1]. RUNX2 is involved in osteogenesis and chondrocyte maturation [1]. A FOS-RUNX2 gene fusion was reported in osteoblastoma samples [1]. Runx2 (Cbfa1) is a master switch for osteoblast differentiation from mesenchymal progenitor cells [2]. In compressed periodontal tissue, bone morphogenic proteins and Runx2 expression are induced, and bone morphogenic proteins and Runx2 potentiate osteoblast differentiation and bone mineralization [2]. Runx2 directs multipotent mesenchymal cells to osteoblast-lineage cells by inducing Sp7 and converting Runx2+ osteoprogenitors into Runx2+Sp7+ preosteoblasts [3]. Canonical Wnt signaling is required for Runx2+Sp7+ preosteoblasts to differentiate into immature osteoblasts expressing Col1a1 and Spp1 [3]. In multiple myeloma, RUNX2 expression is increased in myeloma cells and promotes myeloma-driven bone destruction [5]. RUNX2 upregulation suppresses osteoblast activity and enhances osteoclast activity via regulation of TGFβ, MMP-9, ITGA5, or RANKL [5].
