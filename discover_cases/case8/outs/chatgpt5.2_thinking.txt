RUNX2 (also called Cbfa1/Osf2 in early studies) is functionally linked to osteoblast biology as an osteoblast-lineage transcription factor and regulator of osteoblast differentiation. In mice with targeted disruption of Cbfa1/Runx2, skeletal examination showed a complete lack of ossification, and the authors concluded that both intramembranous and endochondral ossification were completely blocked due to maturational arrest of osteoblasts (Komori et al., 1997). In an independent Cbfa1/Runx2-deficient mouse model, analysis of the skeleton reported an absence of osteoblasts and bone, while heterozygous mice exhibited skeletal abnormalities characteristic of cleidocranial dysplasia (Otto et al., 1997). Mechanistically, Osf2/Cbfa1 was cloned as the protein that binds the osteoblast-specific cis-acting element OSE2 in the osteocalcin promoter, and Osf2/Cbfa1 was reported to bind to and regulate expression of multiple osteoblast-expressed genes (Ducy et al., 1997). That same study reported that Osf2/Cbfa1 expression becomes restricted to cells of the osteoblast lineage during development and that forced expression of Osf2/Cbfa1 in nonosteoblastic cells induces expression of principal osteoblast-specific genes (Ducy et al., 1997).
