## RUNX2 — osteoblasts or osteoblast differentiation

**Query:** What functional relationships between RUNX2 and osteoblasts or osteoblast differentiation have been directly reported in peer-reviewed research articles?

RUNX2 is a crucial transcription factor for osteoblast differentiation [0] and regulates the expression of major bone matrix protein genes, including Col1a1, Spp1, Ibsp, Bglap2, and Fn1 [0]. RUNX2 is essential for osteoblast differentiation and chondrocyte maturation [0]. It regulates the proliferation of osteoblast progenitors by directly regulating Fgfr2 and Fgfr3 [0]. Moreover, RUNX2 induces the expression of major bone matrix protein genes, including Col1a1, Spp1, Ibsp, Bglap2, and Fn1, in vitro [0]. In vivo, RUNX2 regulates the expression of these genes in osteoblasts, and its deficiency leads to impaired bone formation [8]. The CK2/HAUSP pathway stabilizes RUNX2 and regulates the commitment of skeletal stem cells to osteoprogenitors and their subsequent maturation [5]. Additionally, RUNX2 is required for the expression of major bone matrix protein genes and Tnfsf11 after commitment into osteoblasts [8]. Furthermore, RUNX2 is a molecular sensor of the osteoblast REDOX environment and mechanistically clarifies how ROS negatively impacts osteoblast differentiation and bone formation [3]. VGLL4 relieves TEADs-mediated transcriptional inhibition of RUNX2 to promote osteoblast differentiation and bone development [4]. RUNX2 regulates the BMP4 pathway by inhibiting CHRDL1 transcription [2]. The FXR receptor stabilizes RUNX2 by inhibiting Thoc6-mediated ubiquitination, promoting osteogenic activity in BMSCs [6]. Lastly, tripeptide IRW initiates differentiation in osteoblasts via the RUNX2 pathway [9]. The regulation of RUNX2 by post-translational modifications, such as phosphorylation, acetylation, and ubiquitination, also plays a crucial role in controlling bone homeostasis [7]. In summary, RUNX2 is essential for osteoblast differentiation, and its dysregulation can lead to various bone disorders [9]. Understanding the functional relationships between RUNX2 and osteoblasts is crucial for the development of novel therapeutic strategies for bone diseases.
