RUNX2 (Runt-related transcription factor 2) is a master transcriptional regulator of osteoblast differentiation, essential for the commitment of mesenchymal progenitor cells to the osteoblast lineage (Komori et al., 1997; Otto et al., 1997). RUNX2-null mice exhibit a complete absence of bone formation due to maturational arrest of osteoblasts, demonstrating that RUNX2 is indispensable for both intramembranous and endochondral ossification (Komori et al., 1997). RUNX2 directly binds to the osteoblast-specific cis-acting element (OSE2) in the promoter of the osteocalcin gene, activating its transcription and thereby regulating osteoblast-specific gene expression (Ducy et al., 1997). Beyond osteocalcin, RUNX2 regulates the expression of other bone matrix genes including type I collagen, osteopontin, and bone sialoprotein in osteoblast lineage cells (Ducy et al., 1999). Importantly, while RUNX2 is required for early osteoblast differentiation, its constitutive overexpression in mature osteoblasts inhibits late-stage osteoblast maturation, indicating that RUNX2 must be downregulated for terminal differentiation to proceed (Liu et al., 2001). RUNX2 activity in osteoblasts is further modulated by post-translational modifications and interactions with co-regulators such as CBFβ, which enhances RUNX2 DNA-binding stability (Yoshida et al., 2002).
