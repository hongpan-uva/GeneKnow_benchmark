## FOXR2 — neuroblastoma, including NB-FOXR2

**Query:** What functional relationships between FOXR2 and neuroblastoma, including NB-FOXR2, have been directly reported in peer-reviewed research articles?

The discovery of FOXR2 as an oncogene for medulloblastoma and CNS neuroblastoma with FOXR2 activation (NB-FOXR2) has led to a deeper understanding of its functional relationships with neuroblastoma. A study by [1] showed that FOXR2 promotes the formation of CNS-embryonal tumors in a Trp53-deficient background. FOXR2 was found to stabilize MYCN protein and increase MYCN protein levels, making it an alternative mechanism to MYCN amplification for tumor initiation [0]. This study demonstrated that FOXR2-expressing tumors are very similar to MYCN-amplified tumors, suggesting a common mechanism of tumor initiation [0]. Another study by [2] revealed that FOXR2 interacts with N-MYC, increases C-MYC protein stability, and activates FAK/SRC signaling. The same study showed that FOXR2 is a putative proto-oncogene for medulloblastoma and CNS-PNET. Additionally, FOXR2 was found to be associated with a subset of human embryonal tumors of the CNS, designated as CNS neuroblastoma with FOXR2 activation (CNS NB-FOXR2) [1]. Furthermore, a study by [5] demonstrated that FOXR2 is a diagnostic marker for CNS neuroblastoma with FOXR2 activation (CNS NB-FOXR2), and its expression identifies a group of patients with unfavorable clinical outcome, distinct from MYCN-amplified tumors [0]. Another study by [7] established SOX10 and ANKRD55 expression as genes discriminating CNS NB-FOXR2 from other tumors exhibiting CNS-PNET-like microscopic appearance. In summary, FOXR2 has been directly reported to stabilize MYCN protein, promote tumor formation, and interact with other oncogenes like N-MYC [1][2], making it a critical factor in the development and progression of neuroblastoma, including NB-FOXR2 [0].
