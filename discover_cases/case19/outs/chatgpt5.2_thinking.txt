FOXR2 has been functionally implicated in neuroblastoma through clinical association and mechanistic studies showing that FOXR2 can act upstream of MYCN protein abundance. In a cohort study of primary neuroblastoma tumors, FOXR2 expression identified a subset of **non–MYCN-amplified** neuroblastomas with **unfavorable outcome** and was reported as an **independent prognostic factor** relative to established risk variables. (Schmitt-Hoffner et al., 2021) In the same work, FOXR2 was shown to **bind MYCN** and **increase/stabilize MYCN protein levels** (providing an alternative route to high MYCN protein without MYCN gene amplification). (Schmitt-Hoffner et al., 2021) Functional perturbation experiments in FOXR2-expressing neuroblastoma models reported that reducing FOXR2 led to **decreased MYCN protein** and impaired tumor-cell growth phenotypes (e.g., growth inhibition/cell-cycle effects), supporting a dependency of this subgroup on FOXR2–MYCN regulation. (Schmitt-Hoffner et al., 2021) Separately, pediatric **central nervous system neuroblastoma with FOXR2 activation** has been described as a distinct molecular entity emerging from tumors previously classified as CNS-PNET, with FOXR2 activation used as a defining molecular feature in that diagnostic context. (Korshunov et al., 2021)
