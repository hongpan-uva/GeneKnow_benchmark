## NEUROD1 — neurons or neuronal differentiation

**Query:** What functional relationships between NEUROD1 (NEUROD) and neurons or neuronal differentiation have been directly reported in peer-reviewed research articles?

[Response_Start] NEUROD1 (NEUROD) has been directly linked to various aspects of neuronal development and differentiation. Key findings include its role in initiating differentiation of induced pluripotent stem cells into neural progenitor cells [1], promoting axonal regeneration in adult spinal neurons [2], and reprogramming Müller cells into retinal neurons in adult mice [3]. Additionally, NeuroD1 administration has been found to promote neurogenesis and alleviate neurocognitive dysfunction in a mouse model of subarachnoid hemorrhage [4]. Furthermore, NeuroD1 has been implicated in the regulation of microRNA-124, which plays a crucial role in the transition states of neuronal development [6]. In vivo neuronal reprogramming using NeuroD1 has also been shown to regenerate spinal cord neurons from reactive astrocytes after spinal cord injury [7]. Moreover, NeuroD1 has been identified as an intrinsic factor for neuronal differentiation through induction of proneural genes, including POU3F2 and NEUROD1 [8]. Lastly, miR-30a-5p and miR-153-3p have been found to regulate NeuroD1 and reduce LPS-induced neuroinflammatory response and neuronal apoptosis [9]. These findings demonstrate the importance of NeuroD1 in neuronal development and differentiation, and highlight its potential as a therapeutic target for neurological disorders.
