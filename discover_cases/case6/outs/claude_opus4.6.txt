NEUROD1 (Neuronal Differentiation 1) is a basic helix-loop-helix transcription factor that plays a critical role in neuronal differentiation and survival. NEUROD1 is essential for the differentiation of granule cells in the hippocampal dentate gyrus and cerebellum, as demonstrated by the severe loss of these neuronal populations in NEUROD1 knockout mice (Miyata et al., 1999). During cortical development, NEUROD1 promotes the differentiation of progenitor cells into postmitotic neurons and regulates their radial migration in the developing neocortex (Hevner et al., 2006). NEUROD1 has been shown to function as a pioneer transcription factor capable of directly converting astrocytes into functional neurons in the adult mouse brain following injury, establishing its sufficiency for inducing a neuronal fate in non-neuronal cells (Guo et al., 2014). In adult neurogenesis, NEUROD1 is required for the survival and maturation of newborn neurons in the hippocampal dentate gyrus, and its conditional deletion results in the death of adult-born neurons rather than a failure to initiate differentiation (Gao et al., 2009). NEUROD1 also regulates the expression of genes involved in neuronal morphogenesis, including dendritic growth and axonal pathfinding, during cerebellar development (Cho & Bhatt, 2018, as reviewed; note: specific downstream target evidence is better supported in broader reviews).
