NEUROD1

NEUROD1 acts as a transcription factor that activates neuronal gene expression [1-3]. NEUROD1 promotes conversion of non-neuronal somatic cells into neurons [1,3-5]. NEUROD1 is required for neuronal development and survival [2,5]. For example, in somatic-to-neuron conversion, upstream proteins activate NEUROD1 [1]. NEUROD1 functions as a transcription factor that directly binds promoters and enhancers of neuronal genes and thereby overrides pluripotency to promote neurogenesis [2]. In mice NEUROD1 is required for normal neurogenesis in sensory ganglia and for survival of developing neurons [2]. In injured mouse cortex post-stroke, AAV NeuroD1 induces astrocyte-to-neuron conversion [4]. Converted neurons are NeuN-positive and express pyramidal markers [4]. Converted neurons increase peri-injury neuronal density [4]. At 60 dpi converted neurons fire action potentials and show excitatory and inhibitory synaptic events [4]. NEUROD1 is required for spiral ganglion neuron development [5]. NEUROD1 negatively regulates Atoh1 [5]. Loss of NEUROD1 in vestibular ganglion produces Atoh1-positive hair-cell–like cells, and a NEUROD1–Atoh1 interaction is required for this neuronal conversion [5]. NEUROD1 was used to generate neurons from T cells under glial-free conditions by helping overcome epigenetic barriers with OCT3/4, SOX2, KLF4, and c-MYC [3]. The combination generated glutamatergic neurons and supported direct conversion from peripheral blood without iPSC intermediates [3].
