FOXP3

FOXP3 is a lineage-defining transcription factor that regulates regulatory T cell development [1-3]. FOXP3 also defines regulatory T cells that mediate immune suppression and tolerance [1,4,5]. For example, FOXP3-expressing regulatory T cells suppress immunity in tumors by forming physical, metabolic, and trafficking barriers that exclude pro-inflammatory cells [4]. Induction of regulatory T cell fragility is associated with lower FOXP3 expression and with enhanced interferon-gamma production [4]. FOXP3 is the lineage-defining transcription factor that regulates regulatory T cell development [1]. In tumours FOXP3+ Treg are immunosuppressive and promote immune escape [1]. Bacteria-driven expansion of pro‑inflammatory FOXP3(lo) non‑Treg cells enhances antitumour immunity [1]. In thymus, cytokine-dependent upregulation of FOXP3 in CD25+ progenitors constitutes the second developmental step and produces mature Tregs that promote tolerance [3]. In periphery, repeated antigen exposure with IL-2 and TGF-β drives conversion of Foxp3- cells into Foxp3+ pTregs [3]. FOXP3+ regulatory T cells produce FOXP3 and exert immunosuppressive effects that regulate immunity and tumor biology [5]. Post‑translational modifications and non‑coding RNAs activate FOXP3 signaling, enabling FOXP3+ Treg function in tumors [5]. Foxp3 is the master transcription factor that regulates regulatory T cell development by forming complexes that activate or repress genes for regulatory T cell differentiation and function [2]. Foxp3 deficiency causes lethal systemic autoimmunity in scurfy mice and human IPEX [2].
