Article Summary

APOE4 is the single greatest genetic risk factor for sporadic Alzheimer’s disease. Researchers studied APOE4 in CRISPR/Cas9-edited human iPSC-derived brain cells and in isogenic human iPSC-derived neurons, astrocytes, and microglia-like cells. Transcriptional profiling found cell-type-specific expression changes affecting synaptic function in neurons, lipid metabolism in astrocytes, and immune response in microglia-like cells. In neurons, APOE4 was associated with increased synapse number and elevated Aβ42 secretion versus isogenic APOE3. In astrocytes, APOE4 was associated with impaired Aβ uptake and cholesterol accumulation. In microglia-like cells, APOE4 was associated with altered morphologies that correlated with reduced Aβ phagocytosis. Conversion of APOE4 to APOE3 in sporadic AD patient-derived iPSCs alleviated most AD-related phenotypes in neurons, glia, and organoids. However, Aβ42 secretion was not reduced in APOE3 versus APOE4 sporadic AD neurons. Reduction in early endosome numbers in APOE3 sporadic AD neurons was not statistically significant despite a trend versus APOE4. The authors reported that additional factors in that sporadic AD iPSC-derived cell line may confound the observed AD-related pathologies.

The apolipoprotein E4 ( APOE4 ) variant is the single greatest genetic risk factor for sporadic Alzheimer’s disease (sAD). However, the cell type-specific functions of APOE4 in relation to AD pathology remain understudied. Here, we utilize CRISPR/Cas9 and induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) to examine APOE4 effects on human brain cell types. Transcriptional profiling identified hundreds of differentially expressed genes in each cell type, with the most affected involving synaptic function (neurons), lipid metabolism (astrocytes) and immune response (microglia-like cells). APOE4 neurons exhibited increased synapse number and elevated Aβ 42 secretion relative to isogenic APOE3 cells while APOE4 astrocytes displayed impaired Aβ uptake and cholesterol accumulation. Notably, APOE4 microglia-like cells exhibited altered morphologies, which correlated with reduced Aβ phagocytosis. Consistently, converting APOE4 to APOE3 in brain cell types from sAD iPSCs was sufficient to attenuate multiple AD-related pathologies. Our study establishes a reference for human cell type-specific changes associated with the APOE4 variant.
