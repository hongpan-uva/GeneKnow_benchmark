KLF4

KLF4 functions as a tumor suppressor in colorectal cancer [1-3]. KLF4 inhibits WNT signaling in colorectal cancer [1,2]. In F. nucleatum–associated late-stage CRC, Ca2+-driven E-cadherin–KLF4 interaction causes KLF4 nuclear translocation, inducing ITGA5 transcription and promoting tumor growth and metastasis [1]. KLF4 dampens pre-metastatic niche formation and metastasis in colorectal cancer [4]. Colorectal cancer exosomal miR-25-3p downregulates endothelial KLF4 [4]. Endothelial KLF4 downregulation correlates with decreased KLF2 and tight-junction proteins and with increased VEGFR2 [4]. miR-25-3p promotes angiogenesis, vascular permeability and metastasis in lung and liver [4]. KLF4 functions as a terminal differentiation regulator in colorectal cancer [2]. KLF4 inhibits WNT signaling by competitively interacting with β-catenin, and suppression of KLF4 activates WNT signaling to sustain CRC cells in a rapidly dividing undifferentiated state [2]. KLF4 is downregulated in human colorectal cancer [3]. KLF4 inhibits CRC growth by upregulating p21 and NDRG2 and by downregulating cyclin D1 [3]. KLF4 deficiency or microRNA-mediated downregulation promotes proliferation, migration, EMT, and tumor progression [3]. In colorectal cancer, KLF4 regulates COL12A1 [5]. KLF4 overexpression in HCT116 CRC cells reduced COL12A1 [5]. KLF4 upregulation associates with glycolysis suppression in CRC [5]. Caffeine upregulated KLF4 across CRC cell lines and decreased COL12A1 [5].
