Krüppel-like factor 4 (KLF4) predominantly functions as a tumor suppressor in colorectal cancer (CRC), with research demonstrating its expression is significantly downregulated in human CRC tissues and cell lines compared to healthy colonic mucosa (Zhao et al., 2004; Wei et al., 2005). KLF4 exerts antiproliferative effects by inducing the expression of the cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitor p21^WAF1/CIP1^ (Zhao et al., 2004). Mechanistically, KLF4 interacts directly with $\beta$-catenin and the T-cell factor 4 (TCF4) complex to block the recruitment of the coactivator p300/CBP, thereby inhibiting the pro-tumorigenic Wnt/$\beta$-catenin signaling pathway (Evans et al., 2010). *In vivo* studies using mouse models confirm that KLF4 suppresses the development of colonic neoplasia (Ghaleb et al., 2016). However, its function is context-dependent; following $\gamma$-radiation-induced DNA damage, KLF4 exhibits anti-apoptotic activity in CRC cells by activating p21^WAF1/CIP1^ and repressing p53-mediated transactivation of the pro-apoptotic gene *BAX* (Ghaleb et al., 2006). No direct evidence currently supports a primary oncogenic role for wild-type KLF4 in the development or progression of colorectal epithelial tumors.
