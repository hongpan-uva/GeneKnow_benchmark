## KLF4 — colorectal cancer (CRC)

**Query:** What functional relationships between KLF4 and colorectal cancer (CRC) have been directly reported in peer-reviewed research articles?

The research articles highlight that KLF4 has tumor suppressor properties in colorectal cancer (CRC). KLF4 functions as a tumor suppressor by inhibiting epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT), promoting differentiation, and suppressing cancer stem cell properties [2][3][8]. KLF4 targets and regulates various genes involved in these processes, such as SOX2, RAB26, and MMP2 [4][5][8]. Tumor Suppressor Properties: KLF4 has been found to be downregulated in CRC tissues and cell lines, and its expression is negatively correlated with cancer stem cell properties and metastasis [1][4]. KLF4 overexpression has been shown to inhibit cell proliferation, migration, and invasion in CRC cells, while its downregulation promotes these processes [5][8]. Regulation of Cancer Stem Cell Properties: KLF4 has been implicated in the regulation of cancer stem cell properties in CRC. KLF4 is a direct target of miR-29a, miR-543, and other microRNAs, which highlights its role in regulating CRC development and metastasis [4][5][9]. KLF4 downregulation promotes cancer stem cell-like properties, while its overexpression inhibits these properties [4][5]. Regulation of Chemotherapy Resistance: KLF4 has also been implicated in the regulation of chemotherapy resistance in CRC. KLF4 is downregulated in oxaliplatin-resistant CRC cells, and its overexpression can restore oxaliplatin sensitivity [3]. KLF4 also targets RAB26 to inhibit autophagy and decrease 5-FU resistance in CC cells [8]. Overall, the research articles demonstrate that KLF4 plays a crucial role in regulating CRC development, progression, and metastasis, and its dysregulation is associated with poor prognosis. Targeting KLF4 may represent a promising therapeutic strategy for the treatment of CRC.
