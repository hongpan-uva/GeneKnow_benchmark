In colorectal cancer (CRC), **KLF4 has been reported to be reduced and functionally linked to growth- and Wnt-related phenotypes in specific experimental systems**. In paired human specimens, **KLF4 mRNA was reported to be lower in CRC than matched normal colonic tissue (mean 52% of normal across 30 pairs)**, and KLF4 mRNA/protein levels were also reported to be lower in several CRC cell lines than in an untransformed colonic epithelial line (Zhao et al., 2004). In a genetic intestinal tumor model, **Klf4 haploinsufficiency was reported to increase adenoma burden**: **Klf4(+/−)/Apc(Min/+) mice developed ~59% more intestinal adenomas than Apc(Min/+) controls (10–20 weeks)** (Ghaleb et al., 2007). Mechanistically, in cell-based assays, **KLF4 was reported to directly interact with β-catenin’s C-terminal transactivation domain and inhibit β-catenin–dependent recruitment of p300/CBP, reducing β-catenin/TCF transcriptional output** (Evans et al., 2010). Separately, **pVHL was reported to physically interact with KLF4 and promote its degradation; pVHL depletion in colorectal cancer cells was reported to cause cell-cycle arrest with increased transcription of the KLF4-dependent p21 gene, and immunohistochemistry was reported to show elevated pVHL with reduced KLF4 in colon cancer tissues** (Gamper et al., 2012). Clinically, **KLF4 protein expression was reported to correlate with histologic differentiation in CRC** (Hu et al., 2011).
