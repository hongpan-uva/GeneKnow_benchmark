FOXA1

FOXA1 functions as a transcriptional regulator in prostate cancer [1-3]. FOXA1 functions as a pioneer factor in luminal prostate epithelium and in prostate cancer [4]. FOXA1 mutations alter its pioneering activity and perturb luminal epithelial differentiation [4]. FOXA1 mutation-driven perturbation of luminal epithelial differentiation prompts prostate cancer progression [4]. In prostate cancer FOXA1 orchestrates splicing dysregulation by binding regulatory regions and controlling splicing-factor expression [1]. FOXA1 exploits exon-definition to bias dominant over NMD-targeted isoforms [1]. FOXA1-controlled SRSF1 promotes FLNA exon 30 inclusion [1]. FLNA exon 30 inclusion promotes in vitro growth [1]. FLNA exon 30 inclusion predicts recurrence [1]. FOXA1 targets the androgen receptor in prostate cancer [2]. Missense mutations in FOXA1's winged-helix likely disrupt cofactor interactions rather than DNA contact and define a subtype with elevated AR-induced transcription [2]. FOXA1 is recurrently mutated in prostate cancer [3]. Germline SNPs and somatic SNVs modulate FOXA1 binding and transcriptional regulation by altering binding at cis-regulatory elements [3]. FOXA1 3' UTR indels appear in mCRPC plasma DNA and enable liquid-biopsy detection and longitudinal monitoring [3]. Whole-exome sequencing of seven human prostate cancer organoid lines identified disease-specific FOXA1 mutations [5]. FOXA1 mutations are listed among genetic lesions that underlie prostate tumorigenesis [5]. FOXA1-mutant tumors are not represented in the limited available prostate cancer cell lines [5].
