The *Transcription Factor 7-Like 2 (TCF7L2)* gene is the most robust genetic risk locus identified for Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM) to date (Jin & Liu, 2008; Madhu et al., 2022). Functionally, *TCF7L2* operates as a critical effector within the canonical Wnt signaling pathway, where it regulates gene expression essential for pancreatic beta-cell function and systemic glucose homeostasis (Jin & Liu, 2008). In the pancreas, the downregulation of *TCF7L2* protein leads to impaired glucose-stimulated insulin secretion and the direct suppression of receptors for the incretin hormones, specifically the glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor (*GLP1R*) and the gastric inhibitory polypeptide receptor (*GIPR*) (Shu et al., 2009). This downregulation diminishes the incretin-induced potentiation of insulin release; notably, while circulating GLP-1 levels may remain within normal physiological ranges in risk-allele carriers, the cellular response to GLP-1 is significantly impaired (Shu et al., 2009). Furthermore, *TCF7L2* modulates hepatic glucose metabolism by regulating gluconeogenesis and the expression of the *proglucagon* gene in intestinal endocrine cells (Jin & Liu, 2008). Emerging evidence also implicates *TCF7L2* in systemic lipid metabolism, as risk-allele carriers exhibit significant postprandial triglyceride dysmetabolism and upregulated gene expression in visceral adipose tissue (Madhu et al., 2022). These findings establish *TCF7L2* as a pleiotropic regulator coordinating glucose and lipid homeostasis across the pancreas, liver, intestine, and adipose tissue.
