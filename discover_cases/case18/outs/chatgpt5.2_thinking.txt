Common intronic variation in **TCF7L2** was identified as conferring increased **type 2 diabetes (T2D/T2DM)** risk in human genetic association analyses (Grant et al., 2006). In the Diabetes Prevention Program cohort of individuals with impaired glucose tolerance, **TCF7L2** polymorphisms were associated with **progression to diabetes**, and the risk genotypes were reported to be associated with **impaired β-cell function** rather than increased insulin resistance at baseline (Florez et al., 2006). In metabolic phenotyping of humans stratified by **TCF7L2** genotype, the risk allele was associated with **impaired insulin secretion**, **reduced incretin effect**, and an **enhanced rate of hepatic glucose production**, and **TCF7L2 mRNA** was reported to be higher in human islets from donors with T2D; experimentally, **overexpression of TCF7L2 in human islets** was reported to reduce glucose-stimulated insulin secretion (Lyssenko et al., 2007). In carriers of **TCF7L2** polymorphisms, GLP-1–stimulated insulin secretion was reported to be impaired (Schäfer et al., 2009). In isolated pancreatic islet β-cells, experimental manipulation of **TCF7L2** was reported to regulate late events in insulin secretion (da Silva Xavier et al., 2009).
