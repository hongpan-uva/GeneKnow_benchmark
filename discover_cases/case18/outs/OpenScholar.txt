## TCF7L2 — diabetes mellitus, including type 2 diabetes (T2D or T2DM)

**Query:** What functional relationships between TCF7L2 and diabetes mellitus, including type 2 diabetes (T2D or T2DM), have been directly reported in peer-reviewed research articles?

Response_Start The transcription factor 7-like 2 (TCF7L2) gene has been directly associated with an increased risk of developing type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) through various functional relationships. The most significant association was found in a meta-analysis of 56628 participants, which showed that the rs7903146 variant of TCF7L2 is significantly associated with T2DM in Caucasian, East Asian, South Asian, and other ethnicities [3]. This association has been consistently replicated in several studies, including those conducted in the Lebanese [2], Iranian [4], and Moroccan populations [7]. Furthermore, the rs7903146 variant has been shown to be associated with an increased risk of T2DM in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa population of Pakistan [5] and the Moroccan population [7]. TCF7L2 has also been linked to diabetic microvascular complications. A study conducted in Thai subjects with T2DM found that the specific combination of risk alleles in TCF7L2 and KCNQ1 genes was associated with an increased risk of coronary artery disease (CAD), cumulative nephropathy, and cumulative microvascular and macrovascular complications [6]. However, no significant association was found between TCF7L2 polymorphisms and diabetic complications in a study conducted in diabetic patients without complications and diabetic patients with microvascular complications [1]. Moreover, the TCF7L2 gene plays a complex role in human adipose progenitor biology, which might contribute to genetic susceptibility to type 2 diabetes. The gene is expressed in adipose progenitors and mature adipocytes, and its expression is associated with adipogenesis and insulin sensitivity [8]. The T2D-risk variant at rs7903146 was associated with reduced TCF7L2 expression in adipose progenitors and enhanced adipose tissue insulin sensitivity. The association of TCF7L2 with type 1 diabetes is also worth mentioning. A study found that the TCF7L2 rs7903146 variant was associated with single islet autoantibody expression in children with type 1 diabetes, suggesting that TCF7L2 might contribute to the development of type 1 diabetes [9]. In conclusion, the TCF7L2 gene has been directly associated with an increased risk of developing T2DM and diabetic microvascular complications through various functional relationships, including its role in adipose progenitor biology and insulin sensitivity [8]. The rs7903146 variant is a significant risk factor for T2DM in various populations, and the gene's expression is associated with adipogenesis and insulin sensitivity [8]. Response_End
