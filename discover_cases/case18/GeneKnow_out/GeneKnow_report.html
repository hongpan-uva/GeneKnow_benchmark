TCF7L2

TCF7L2 functions as a genetic susceptibility factor for type 2 diabetes mellitus [1-3]. TCF7L2 acts as a regulator of pancreatic islet beta-cell biology and is primarily expressed in islet beta cells [2,4]. TCF7L2 gene variants contribute to type 2 diabetes by impairing insulin secretion and pancreatic β-cell function [4]. Variant rs290487 contributed to insulin resistance and implicated Wnt signaling [4]. The rs7903146T allele in TCF7L2 is a replicated T2DM susceptibility variant and a top GWAS signal [1]. rs7903146T influences impaired insulin secretion [1]. In a Kuwaiti sample the TT genotype associated with increased T2DM risk and the variant associated with peripheral neuropathy and myocardial infarction [1]. In gestational diabetes, TCF7L2 may affect pancreatic β-cells, reducing insulin and glucagon-like peptide secretion and increasing endogenous glucose [5]. In type 2 diabetes, TCF7L2 may interfere with GLP1, reduce GLP1R and GIP-R expression and activate Wnt signaling [5]. TCF7L2 is the strongest susceptibility gene for T2DM and is mainly expressed in islet beta cells [2]. TCF7L2 variation is linked to decreased insulin secretion due to reduced pancreatic β‑cell function [2]. Variants including rs7901695 correlate with T2DM risk [2]. Common variants in TCF7L2 were linked to susceptibility to type 2 diabetes mellitus [3]. rs7903146 in TCF7L2 carries allele T as the reported T2DM risk allele [3]. TCF7L2 association with T2DM replicated across many populations but not in Chinese [3].
