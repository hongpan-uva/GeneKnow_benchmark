Article Summary

TCF7L2 gene variants contribute to the pathogenesis of type 2 diabetes and predict disease onset in human cohorts. The genetic association has been consistently replicated across diverse populations and a meta-analysis indicates a multiplicative genetic model. These variants are estimated to be involved in nearly one-fifth of T2D cases. Variant rs290487 contributed to insulin resistance and implicated Wnt signaling. In a French cohort, carriers of TCF7L2 gene variants showed increased glycemia, decreased insulin secretion, and a more rapid reduction of pancreatic β-cell function. The effects observed in carriers of TCF7L2 variants in the French cohort (more rapid reduction of β-cell function, increased glycemia, decreased insulin secretion) were related to familial history of type 2 diabetes. The role of TCF7L2 variants in complex regulatory functions remains little explored. Resequencing of the TCF7L2 gene in diverse ethnic groups will allow identification of common and rare genetic variants that contribute to the pathogenesis of type 2 diabetes. Therapies that modulate TCF7L2 expression may offer treatment opportunities for risk-allele carriers.

Additional studies in different cohorts worldwide have detected the role of the TCF7L2 risk variants in glucose metabolism and T2D. In 2007, the Finnish Diabetes Prevention Study showed that some TCF7L2 variants predict T2D onset and are associated with impaired glucose regulation and insulin secretion ( 34 ). In 2011, the French cohort data from the Epidemiological Study on the Insulin Resistance Syndrome showed the impact of TCF7L2 genetic risk on the evolution of T2D: TCF7L2 variants were associated with a more rapid reduction of pancreatic β-cell function, increased glycemia, and decreased insulin secretion. These effects related to familial history of T2D, and TCF7L2 variants indicate the familial genetic predisposition to the development over time of impaired glycemia and T2D ( 35 ). Physiological studies found that variant rs290487 contributed to insulin resistance, implicating Wnt signaling in insulin resistance ( 36 ).
