Article Summary

TCF7L2 is a high mobility group box-containing transcription factor and a critical component of Wnt signaling. The TCF7L2 gene product acts through regulation of proglucagon gene expression in enteroendocrine cells via the Wnt signaling pathway. In human islets, TCF7L2 expression associates positively with insulin gene expression. Large-scale association studies linked common variants in TCF7L2 to susceptibility to type 2 diabetes mellitus, and a meta-analysis was performed because study quality varied and results were inconsistent across populations. The single-nucleotide polymorphism rs7903146 carries allele T as the reported risk allele for T2DM. Grant et al. reported strong association of common variants in TCF7L2 with T2DM in Icelandic subjects and replication occurred in Danish and U.S. cohorts. The T-allele of rs7903146 was reported to increase T2DM risk in French, Japanese, African American, and Brasilia populations. Zheng et al. found no association between rs7903146 and T2DM in the Chinese population.
