CEBPA

CEBPA promotes granulocytic lineage specification and neutrophil differentiation [1-3]. CEBPA regulates myeloid differentiation and when mutated or repressed contributes to differentiation arrest in acute myeloid leukemia (AML) [2-4]. C/EBPα is required for generation of Ly6G+ neutrophils and Ly6Chi monocytes in cultured cKithi bone marrow progenitors when Notch signaling is absent [1]. C/EBPα maintains Ly6Chi monocyte identity by halting progression to Ly6Clo monocytes [1]. CEBPA activates HK3 and KLF5 during neutrophil differentiation, directly regulating HK3 and indirectly regulating KLF5 [2]. C/EBPα is required for formation of granulocyte-monocyte progenitors from common myeloid progenitors [3]. HoxA9 directly binds and inhibits the +8 kb enhancer and represses Cebpa expression [3]. HoxA9 impairs myeloid differentiation in normal HSPCs and in poor-risk AML [3]. CEBPA was upregulated in HSPCs exposed to supernatants from activated CAR-T cells and activity of regulons involved in neutrophil maturation decreased [5]. In one relapse case with biallelic CEBPA mutation, there was expansion of maturing neutrophilic granulopoiesis [4]. The expansion was associated with an increased myeloid-erythroid ratio, near-100% bone marrow cellularity, and approximately 10% blasts [4].
