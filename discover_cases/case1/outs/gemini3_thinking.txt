SOX9 is a transcription factor essential for the specification, maturation, and functional modulation of astrocytes. During development, SOX9 is required for the gliogenic switch and the initiation of astrogenesis; its absence prevents the formation of astrocyte lineages, while its overexpression is sufficient to generate functional astrocytes from human pluripotent stem cells (Neyrinck et al., 2021). In the adult central nervous system, SOX9 serves as a definitive nuclear marker for astrocytes, maintaining stable expression levels that do not diminish with age (Sun et al., 2017). Functionally, SOX9 directly regulates homeostatic activities in mature astrocytes, including the promotion of glutamate uptake and the enhancement of migratory potential (Balint et al., 2024). Under pathological conditions, SOX9 is significantly upregulated in reactive astrocytes following injury or in neurodegenerative models (Sun et al., 2017). Specifically, in Alzheimer’s disease, astrocytic SOX9 promotes the clearance of amyloid-beta plaques by transcriptionally regulating the phagocytic receptor MEGF10 (Choi et al., 2026). Furthermore, nerve injury-induced SOX9 activity has been identified as a driver of neuroinflammatory astrocyte subtypes through the regulation of metabolic genes like Hexokinase 1 (Sox9 regulation..., 2025).
