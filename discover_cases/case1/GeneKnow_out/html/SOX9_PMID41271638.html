Article Summary

Sox9 is an astrocyte specifier that is specifically expressed in naïve astrocytes and upregulated in reactive astrocytes after brain or spinal cord injury. In naïve animals Sox9 maintains basal Hk1 levels. Nerve injury triggers Sox9 phosphorylation that enhances Sox9 nuclear translocation and transcriptional activity. Upstream kinases implicated include cAMP–PKA, MAPK, and PI3K/Akt. Phosphorylated Sox9 causes pathological Hk1 upregulation and aberrant glycolytic flux in astrocytes. Phosphorylated Sox9 contributes to the emergence of the Hk1-mediated neuroinflammatory astrocyte cluster 1. Phosphorylated Sox9 induces Astro 2, which represents a property with neurodegenerative and demyelinating nature. Aberrant Hk1 activation drives high-rate astrocytic glycolysis and leads to excessive lactate production from heightened astrocytic glycolysis. Excess lactate remodels histones via lactylation (H3K9la) and promotes pro-inflammatory and neurotoxic transcriptional modules that reduce beneficial astrocyte populations and induce pathogenic astrocyte properties. The pathogenic astrocyte changes contribute to neuropathic pain. Targeted modulation of the Sox9–Hk1–H3K9la axis specifically dampens neuroinflammatory astrocyte subsets and promotes long-lasting pain relief.

Our studies also identified an important regulatory link between the astrocyte specifier, Sox9 and glycolysis, potentially explaining differential metabolic programs adopted by astrocytes and neurons during development and homeostasis. Sox9 belongs to a member of the SRY-like HMG-box family, which functions as a key transcriptional regulator for the specification of astrocytes, neural crest and chondrocytes during development 9 , 62 . In adults, Sox9 is specifically expressed in naïve astrocytes and upregulated in reactive astrocytes following brain and spinal cord injury 8 , 63 . Previously, we and others found that Sox9 can undergo phosphorylation, leading to distinct effects on cellular behavior and activities 47 . Here, we found that Sox9 maintains basal Hk1 levels in naïve animals, while nerve injury triggers Sox9 phosphorylation that enhances its nuclear translocation and transcriptional activity. This post-translational modification leads to pathological Hk1 upregulation, promoting aberrant glycolytic flux in astrocytes. Phosphorylated Sox9 not only contributes to the emergence of the Hk1-mediated neuroinflammatory astrocyte cluster 1, but also induces Astro 2, which represents a property with neurodegenerative and demyelinating nature. It is plausible that the phosphorylation of Sox9 may trigger different downstream signaling pathways depending on the cellular context, resulting in the development of distinct astrocyte subtypes. Notably, the intimate link between transcriptional regulation of Sox9 and glycolytic changes has also been implicated in a few studies. For example, Sox9 -haploinsufficient mice exhibit metabolic abnormalities with impaired glucose tolerance 64 . The ectopic expression of two key glycolytic enzymes, HK2 and LDHA, has been shown to promote astrocyte differentiation from human neural stem cells via enhanced aerobic glycolysis 42 , resembling the functions of Sox9. It is interesting to note that phosphorylated Sox9 is required to regulate neural crest delamination 47 , with recent research from Bhattacharya et al indicating that delaminating neural crest cells exhibit heightened glycolytic activities to facilitate cell migration 50 , revealing a strong link between phosphorylation of Sox9 and increased cellular glycolytic activity. The interaction between Sox9 and hexokinase mediated glycolytic activities may represent a common regulatory axis in diverse cellular contexts in health or disease. The upstream regulation of Sox9 phosphorylation during pain pathogenesis may involve the activity of one or more protein kinases. For example, the cyclic adenosine monophosphate-protein kinase A (cAMP-PKA), highly involved in maintaining neuropathic pain 65 , has previously been shown to phosphorylate Sox9 during neural crest development. Other kinases, such as the mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) and PI3K/Akt pathways, which are known to regulate reactive astrocytes in chronic pain 2 , have also been associated with Sox9 phosphorylation or expression in cancer 66 . Thus, involvement of posttranslational modification in pain pathogenesis also outlines the potential of targeting specific kinases as a possible therapeutic in the future.

Neuropathic pain, caused by nerve damage, lacks effective treatments. Astrocytes drive chronic neuroinflammation in the dorsal horn, but directly targeting them is challenging due to their heterogeneity and beneficial roles. The regulation of deleterious astrocyte subset emergence in pain is also unknown. Here, we identify distinct astrocyte clusters and elucidate mechanisms by which metabolic regulation of neuroinflammatory astrocyte subsets during pain pathogenesis. We show that the astrocyte specifier Sox9 transcriptionally regulates hexokinase 1 (Hk1), the enzyme that catalyzes the rate-limiting first step of glycolysis. Nerve damage induces abnormal Sox9 phosphorylation, triggering aberrant Hk1 activation for high-rate astrocytic glycolysis. Moreover, the excessive lactate production from heightened glycolysis remodels histones of gene promoters via lactylation, H3K9la, promoting transcriptional modules of pro-inflammatory and neurotoxic genes, which induce pathogenic astrocyte properties while reducing beneficial populations, ultimately causing NeP. Importantly, targeted modulation of the Sox9-Hk1-H3K9la axis specifically dampens neuroinflammatory astrocyte subsets, promoting long-lasting pain relief. Collectively, our findings unveil an important immunometabolic mechanism and identify potential targets for neuropathic pain treatment.
