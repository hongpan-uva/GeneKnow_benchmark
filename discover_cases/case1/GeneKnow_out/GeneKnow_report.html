SOX9

Core role: SOX9 specifies astrocytes [1-3]. Core role: SOX9 marks astrocytic cells and functions as a pan-astrocytic marker [4,5]. For example, Sox9 specifies astrocytes and is upregulated after injury [1]. Injury-induced Sox9 phosphorylation increases Sox9 nuclear activity to upregulate Hk1 and astrocytic glycolysis [1]. Excess lactate lactylates H3K9 and drives pro-inflammatory, neurotoxic astrocyte states causing neuropathic pain [1]. Additionally, SOX9 marks astrocytic cells in IDH1‑mutant grade II oligodendrogliomas and astrocytomas [4]. SOX9+ cells form a population largely distinct from OLIG1+ cells and coexpress APOE, CRYAB, ID4, and KCNN3 with elevated BMP, NOTCH, and MAPK signaling [4]. Moreover, in the studied regions, cells labeled with the pan-astrocytic marker SOX9 were all positive for Rab6A [5]. Rab6A/SOX9 double staining would confirm SOX9 as a pan-astrocytic marker and would support Rab6A as a pan-astrocytic marker [5]. In neural progenitor cells, Sox9 promotes astrocyte and oligodendrocyte differentiation and suppresses neuronal differentiation [2]. Sox9 is required for Zbtb20-driven astrocytogenesis [2]. In embryonic spinal cord, Sox9 with NFIA specifies astrocytes and Sox9 loss impairs glial specification [2]. Finally, SOX9, together with nuclear factor I-A and Notch signaling, mediates astrocyte differentiation [3].
